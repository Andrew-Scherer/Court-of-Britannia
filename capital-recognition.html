Core Premise

There are no realm-wide offices or roles.

All roles‚ÄîKing, Regent, Magistrate, Guards, etc.‚Äîare city-specific only.

The "Capital of Britannia" is not a governing body. It is a recognized center of alignment formed entirely through city-to-city recognition.

The Capital does not rule other cities. It is recognized by them.

The Capital is not a ruler.
It is a city other cities choose to align with.

Definition: Capital Recognition

Capital Recognition is the system by which cities collectively identify which city functions as the political center of Britannia.

A city becomes the Capital only through recognition by other active cities.

Recognition is a political stance, not a mechanical obligation.

City Recognition States

Every Active City-State must exist in exactly one of the following recognition states:

Recognizes a Capital City

The city acknowledges another city as the Capital.

This recognition may accompany fealty, tribute, or protection agreements.

Recognition contributes +1 Capital Recognition to the recognized city.

Recognizes No Capital (Sovereign / Neutral)

The city recognizes no Capital.

Pays no tribute. Receives no capital-level protection.

Contributes 0 recognition to any city.

Recognizes Itself as Capital (Capital Claimant)

The city publicly claims to be the Capital of Britannia.

May solicit recognition from other cities.

Each recognizing city contributes +1 Capital Recognition to the claimant.

Recognition is public, logged, and reversible.

How a City Becomes the Capital

A city becomes the Capital when it meets or exceeds the Capital Recognition Threshold.

Example (illustrative only):

  • Capital Threshold: 3 recognizing cities

Britain begins the game above this threshold by default.

No city is permanently the Capital.

What Capital Status Does

Capital Status does not grant authority over other cities.

Capital Status confers gravitational and procedural effects only, including:

  • Default spawn location for new characters
  • Primary diplomatic destination for other cities
  • Cultural and symbolic legitimacy
  • Hosting of historical and political narrative gravity

The Capital does not appoint officials in other cities.

The Capital does not override other cities' laws or courts.

Loss of Capital Status

A city loses Capital Status when its Capital Recognition falls below the required threshold.

Loss of Capital Status:

  • Requires no conquest
  • Requires no admin intervention
  • Occurs automatically
  • Is publicly recorded

The former Capital becomes a normal city-state immediately.

Claiming Capital Status (Capital Claimants)

Any Active City-State may declare itself a Capital Claimant.

A Capital Claimant:

  • Does not gain Capital effects until recognition threshold is met
  • May be recognized by other cities
  • May compete with existing Capitals through diplomacy, protection, or legitimacy

Multiple claimants may exist simultaneously.

There is no global arbiter.

Relationship to Fealty

Capital Recognition is separate from fealty.

A city may swear fealty without recognizing a Capital (rare but possible).

A city may recognize a Capital without paying tribute.

Fealty agreements define economic and military obligations. Recognition defines legitimacy alignment.

Fealty is contractual. Recognition is ideological.

Population & Residency

Capital Recognition is not based on population size.

Population influences credibility, economic strength, and attractiveness‚Äîbut does not directly determine Capital Status.

This prevents violence from becoming a numerical optimization strategy.

No Realm-Wide Roles

There are no roles that operate above cities.

There is:

  • No Realm King
  • No Realm Magistrate
  • No Realm Guard
  • No Realm Treasury
  • No Realm Offices of any kind

All authority originates from cities.

The Capital is influential only because cities treat it as such.

Authority does not rise above cities.
It accumulates where recognition gathers.

Failure Modes & Consequences

Capital failure results in:

  • Loss of spawn gravity
  • Loss of diplomatic centrality
  • Withdrawal of recognition
  • Increased migration
  • Political fragmentation

These are consequences, not punishments.

Explicit Non-Goals

This system does not:

  • Create empire mechanics
  • Enable city micromanagement
  • Introduce realm-wide administration
  • Allow conquest by population reduction
  • Require constant player oversight

Closing Canon Summary

The Capital is not a ruler.
It is a city other cities choose to align with.

Authority does not rise above cities.
It accumulates where recognition gathers.

When recognition fades, the Capital dissolves.
