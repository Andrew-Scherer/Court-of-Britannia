The Price of Power

Core Function

The Master of the Treasury is the Crown's chief fiscal officer.

They do not rule.
They do not command force.
They do not set salaries.

They shape the economic reality the Crown must govern.

Their authority lies in policy exposure, forecasting, and consequence, not direct control.

Appointment & Removal

Action Authority
Appointed by The King
Removed by The King

During Regency

  • The Regent may appoint or remove the Master of the Treasury only while exercising Crown authority
  • All actions are tagged as Regency Acts and subject to reversal upon coronation

There is typically one Master of the Treasury.

Scarcity is intentional.

Sovereign Boundary (Critical)

The Master of the Treasury is not sovereign.

They propose fiscal policy.
The Crown authorizes it ‚Äî explicitly or implicitly.

Taxation remains a power of the Crown.

1Ô∏è‚É£ Tax Policy Proposals (Input Side Control)

The Master of the Treasury may propose tax rates that affect:

  • Property sales at auction
  • NPC purchased goods
  • Player sold goods through vendors
  • Other system-defined city revenue sources

They do not collect taxes manually.
All taxes are automatic and logged.

2Ô∏è‚É£ The 24-Hour Ratification Rule (Core Mechanic)

Every tax proposal enters a 24-hour review window.

During this window:

The King or acting Regent may:

  • Approve
  • Approve with Modifications
  • Reject

If no Crown action is taken within 24 hours:

The proposal is automatically enacted by default.

This represents:

  • Administrative inertia
  • Crown neglect
  • Silent consent
Ledger Records:
  • Who proposed the policy
  • When the window opened
  • Whether the Crown intervened
  • Whether enactment was explicit or automatic

No proposal can remain pending indefinitely.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Treasury Visibility (Ledger Authority)

The Master of the Treasury has full read access to the Royal Treasury Ledger, including:

  • Current treasury balance
  • Daily inflow (taxes, fees)
  • Daily outflow (wages, bounties, system sinks)
  • Payroll projections
  • Deficit forecasts

This visibility is not power ‚Äî it is accountability.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Insolvency Forecasting & Warnings

The Master may publish formal fiscal warnings, such as:

  • "At current policy, guard payroll exceeds revenue."
  • "Projected partial wages within 72 hours."
  • "Treasury depletion expected within three days."

These warnings:

  • Do not force action
  • Do not block systems
  • Create political pressure
  • Attach accountability to inaction

Ignoring warnings is allowed ‚Äî and consequential.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Recommendation Authority (Not Enforcement)

The Master may recommend:

  • Tax increases or reductions (directly through enacting proposals)
  • Spending restraint (through conversation and play)
  • Guard force reductions (through conversation and play)
  • Salary band adjustments (via constitutional mechanisms)

They cannot enforce these recommendations.

They are the messenger ‚Äî not the executioner.

Relationship to Other Roles

Crown (King / Regent)

  • The Crown owns final authority
  • The Master creates fiscal reality
  • Automatic ratification makes silence expensive
The Treasury proposes. The Crown disposes ‚Äî or is judged for silence.

Magistrate

  • No chain of command
  • Magistrates may respond to fiscal collapse politically
  • The Treasury does not interpret law
  • They intersect only through consequence

Guards & Marshal

  • Guard expansion increases treasury burden automatically
  • Payroll pressure becomes political leverage
  • The Master does not hire, fire, or command guards
  • Guards feel fiscal stress without being directly managed

Citizens & Merchants

Tax policy makes merchants political actors by default:

  • High taxes push trade outside city walls
  • Black markets emerge naturally
  • Merchants lobby the Crown
  • Smuggling becomes economically rational

The Master never creates this manually ‚Äî policy does.

Political Effects of Tax Policy

Tax Rate Effect
High Property Taxes Raises cost of living for residents; reduces auction activity
High Vendor Taxes Creates black markets; facilitates personal player trade outside city
Low Taxes Across Board Encourages trade; may starve treasury and cause wage failures
Differential Rates Incentivizes moving trade outside city walls; accommodates criminal activity

Why This Role Exists (System Purpose)

The Master of the Treasury exists to ensure:

  • Prosperity is felt
  • Collapse is visible
  • Power has a price
  • Inaction creates fallout
  • No one can quietly mismanage the city

They convert numbers into pressure.

Offline Continuity

If no one is online:

  • Taxes continue to apply
  • Ledgers update
  • Payroll expectations form
  • Insolvency clocks tick

When players return:

  • They react to what happened ‚Äî not decide whether it happened

Failure States (All Intentional)

Failure Scenario Outcome
Crown ignores proposal Automatic enactment ‚Üí political backlash
Taxes too high Trade flight ‚Üí black markets ‚Üí unrest
Taxes too low Unpaid roles like guards ‚Üí instability
Treasury collapse Legitimacy crisis ‚Üí political consequence
Design Principle: No failure blocks play. Every failure creates story.

Canon Summary

The Master of the Treasury is an officer of the Crown responsible for fiscal policy, forecasting, and transparency.

They propose taxation and publish financial warnings, but do not command enforcement or set wages.

Tax proposals become law if not challenged by the Crown within twenty-four hours.

Silence is consent. Collapse is remembered.

One-Line Lock-In Rule

The Master controls fiscal truth.
The Crown controls fiscal action.
The realm judges the result.
