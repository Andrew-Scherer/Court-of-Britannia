## Why a Single Faction Leader Is the Wrong Default

A hard faction leader sounds tempting, but it creates problems that cut directly against the design principles.


### 1. It Reintroduces Sovereignty (By Accident)

The moment you name:


- "Faction Leader"
- Bound to a character
- With any mechanical meaning

You've created:


- A de facto sovereign entity
- With a head
- That players expect to matter


> **â— Note**
> That violates: *Factions are non-sovereign. Guilds are sovereign.*


### 2. It Recreates the Steward Problem at the Wrong Layer

If a faction has a leader:


- What happens when they die?
- Defect?
- Go inactive?
- Get captured?

You'd need:


- Succession rules
- Interregnum rules
- Emergency transfers


> Congratulations â€” you just built Guilds 2.0, but worse.


### 3. It Creates a Single-Point-of-Failure Target

If there's a "Faction Leader":


- Assassination becomes optimal
- Politics collapses into gank-hunting
- Coordination dies when one person logs off

That's the opposite of emergent resilience.
