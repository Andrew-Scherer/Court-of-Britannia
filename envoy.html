Hierarchy & Structure

Emissary Chain of Authority

  • King ‚Üí Appoints High Emissary
  • High Emissary (1) ‚Üí Appoints Envoys
  • Envoys (3 maximum) ‚Üí Deliver and witness

Envoys are the working tier. They carry out deliveries, witness responses, and report back. The High Emissary coordinates, escalates, and speaks directly to the King.

Envoys have full authority to act on Britain's behalf. They are not messengers ‚Äî they are representatives.

What Envoys Actually Do

Core Function

Carry Britain's position, not decisions.

Envoys transmit state intent as directed by the High Emissary. They do not negotiate, create policy, or make promises.

They Deliver:

  • Recognition (official acknowledgment of faction legitimacy)
  • Demands (tribute requirements, compliance orders)
  • Warnings (escalation notices, consequences)
  • Ultimatums (final notices before action)

They Witness:

  • Fealty oaths (sworn allegiance to Britain)
  • Withdrawals (revocation of recognition)
  • Public refusals (faction rejections of Britain's authority)

They Report:

  • Faction responses to the High Emissary
  • Witnessed events for the Interfaction Chronicle
  • Incidents, threats, or attacks on their person
  • Requests for escalation or clarification

Why This Role Matters

Envoys Are the Eyes and Ears

  • Presence in the field ‚Äî They travel to faction territories, delivering Britain's will where the High Emissary cannot be present
  • Documentation ‚Äî They create official records of what was said, witnessed, and refused
  • Escalation triggers ‚Äî Their reports determine Britain's next actions

Envoys don't have less authority than the High Emissary in the field ‚Äî they are Britain's authority when delivering proclamations. The difference is organizational: Envoys report to the High Emissary, who coordinates and escalates.

Critical Canon Rule

Harming an Envoy is never "just PvP."
It is a political act Britain must respond to.

This means:

  • Attacking an Envoy creates casus belli
  • Killing an Envoy justifies immediate retaliation
  • Interfering with Envoy business is a crime against Britain
  • PKs have a choice: violence ‚Üí consequences

The Perfect Summary

They are not powerful.
They are dangerous to touch.

Envoys share the same protections as the High Emissary. Harming either is equally serious politically. Britain does not distinguish between attacking a high-ranking or low-ranking representative ‚Äî both are attacks on Britain itself.

What Envoys Are NOT

  • ‚ùå Negotiators (they don't make deals)
  • ‚ùå Diplomats (they don't create policy)
  • ‚ùå Ambassadors (they don't represent faction interests)
  • ‚ùå Messengers (they carry state authority, not personal messages)
  • ‚ùå Subordinate in authority (when delivering, they speak for Britain)
  • ‚ùå Invulnerable (they can be harmed, with consequences)

Powers & Limitations

Powers

  • Deliver official Britain proclamations (as directed by High Emissary)
  • Witness and record faction responses
  • Report directly to the High Emissary
  • Legal protection under Britain's law (equal to High Emissary)
  • Authority to demand audiences with faction leaders

Limitations

  • Cannot make promises or deals
  • Cannot alter Britain's position
  • Cannot recognize or outlaw factions (only deliver such proclamations)
  • Cannot enforce (that's the Guards' role)
  • Cannot retreat from witnessing (refusal to witness is dereliction)
  • Cannot appoint other Envoys (only the High Emissary can)

Optional Visual Markers

For enforcement clarity, Envoys may carry:

  • Tabard (official Britain colors)
  • Royal Seal (proof of authority)
  • Color sash (visible identification)
  • Scroll case (for official documents)

Not mandatory, but it helps prevent "I didn't know." Visual markers should distinguish Envoys from High Emissary (different color sash, smaller seal, etc.) while still showing Britain's authority.

Appointment & Dismissal

Appointment

  • Appointed by: The High Emissary
  • Number: 3 maximum
  • Term: At the High Emissary's pleasure
  • Requirements: Trust, communication skill, willingness to travel

Dismissal

  • Dismissed by: The High Emissary or the King
  • Reasons: Failure to deliver, unauthorized negotiation, corruption, dereliction
  • Effect: Loss of all emissary protections and authority

Relationship with High Emissary

Coordination, Not Hierarchy of Authority

When delivering proclamations, Envoys speak with Britain's full authority. They are not "lesser" representatives ‚Äî they are Britain's voice.

The organizational difference:

  • High Emissary: Coordinates strategy, speaks to the King, decides what gets delivered
  • Envoys: Execute deliveries, witness responses, report back

Think of it this way: The High Emissary is the conductor. Envoys are the musicians. Both are essential. Both carry the music.

How This Creates Gameplay

Field Presence Creates Pressure

Envoys create:

  • Distributed presence ‚Äî 3 Envoys can cover multiple factions simultaneously
  • Escalation flexibility ‚Äî High Emissary can send Envoys for routine matters, appear personally for critical ones
  • Witness redundancy ‚Äî Multiple Envoys mean multiple perspectives on events
  • Target options for factions ‚Äî Attacking an Envoy is politically cheaper than attacking the High Emissary, but still expensive

Example Scenarios

Scenario 1: Routine Tribute Demand

An Envoy travels to a faction base to deliver a tribute demand. The faction leader pays immediately. The Envoy witnesses the payment, records it, and reports success to the High Emissary. Simple, clean, documented.

Scenario 2: Hostile Reception

An Envoy delivers a warning to a faction. The faction leader refuses to meet. The Envoy publicly announces the warning at the faction's gate, witnesses the refusal to appear, and reports. This refusal is recorded in the Interfaction Chronicle as evidence of non-compliance.

Scenario 3: Envoy Attacked

A faction member attacks an Envoy en route to deliver an ultimatum. The Envoy survives and reports to the High Emissary. The High Emissary escalates to the King. Britain declares the attack an act of war and withdraws all recognition. The Envoy's wound becomes casus belli.

Scenario 4: Coordinated Deliveries

The High Emissary directs all 3 Envoys to deliver simultaneous ultimatums to three different factions. This demonstrates Britain's reach and forces coordinated responses. The High Emissary remains in Britain to brief the King on results as they arrive.

Summary

Envoys are Britain's field representatives, appointed by the High Emissary to deliver proclamations, witness faction responses, and report back. They carry the same authority as the High Emissary when delivering Britain's position, but report to the High Emissary organizationally.

Harming an Envoy is never "just PvP" ‚Äî it is a political act that Britain must respond to. Envoys are not powerful people, but they are dangerous to touch.

This role creates distributed presence, escalation flexibility, and witness redundancy without requiring new mechanics. Envoys are the eyes, ears, and voice of Britain in faction territories.
