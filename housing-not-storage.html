Houses Are About Place, Not Vaults

The Core Conclusion

Houses are not vaults.
They are places to live.

Housing does not function as static storage.

Players cannot use housing for:

  • Permanent storage of secured containers
  • Lockdowns that persist indefinitely
  • Hoarding mechanisms
  • Insulating progress from risk

What This Removes

Traditional housing storage creates:

  • Hoarding Anxiety ‚Äî Fear of losing accumulated items creates paranoia and defensiveness
  • Lock/Permission Micromanagement ‚Äî Complex trust systems and access control become necessary
  • Shell-Guild Storage Exploits ‚Äî Players fragment into single-person guilds to maximize storage
  • Housing-as-Entrenchment Gameplay ‚Äî Power accumulates behind walls instead of in the world

By removing housing storage, you eliminate all of these problems.

Housing Grants Access, Not Ownership

How It Works

Living in a building grants access to your global bank box, not ownership of storage containers.

Residency ‚Üí Access to banking services

Personal items are stored through the banking system.

Storage is:

  • Individual ‚Äî Each player has their own bank box
  • Private ‚Äî No one else can access your items
  • Abstracted ‚Äî Not tied to the building itself

Why This Works

This design allows:

  • Shared housing without forced trust
  • Cohabitation without theft risk
  • Political loss without inventory wipeouts
  • Multiple residents without permission complexity
  • Cooperation without vulnerability

What You CAN Do With Housing

Expression, Not Storage

While housing doesn't provide storage, it still allows:

  • Decoration ‚Äî Free access to decorative items
  • Furniture placement ‚Äî Chairs, tables, beds, ambiance
  • Vendors ‚Äî Player-controlled merchants
  • Social gathering ‚Äî A place to meet and roleplay
  • Political presence ‚Äî Visible claim to space

Housing is about where you are, not what you own.

The Shift in Mindset

Players will initially ask:
"If I can't store anything, what's the point of a house?"

The answer:

A house is:
  • A place in the world
  • A social hub
  • A statement of identity
  • A political anchor
  • Access to banking services

Once players adapt, they realize:

  • Less anxiety about loss
  • No paranoia about theft
  • More willingness to invite others
  • Cleaner political conflict
  • Less attachment to buildings, more attachment to people

How This Supports Politics

When housing is divorced from storage:

  • Loss becomes fair ‚Äî Losing a property hurts politically, not personally
  • Cooperation increases ‚Äî No need to create shell guilds for storage
  • Conflict stays social ‚Äî Fights are about territory, not loot
  • Recovery is faster ‚Äî Players re-engage instead of quitting after loss
Housing becomes a stage for politics,
not a vault for accumulation.

The Sacred Line

This rule must be absolute:

If It Affects Power, It's Not In A House

Power-affecting items are stored in banks.

Houses are for presence, expression, and access.

That line keeps the system clean.
