⚔️ Faction Interactions with Other Factions

No Special Mechanics — Only Play, Economics, Violence, and Britain's Reaction

The Short Answer

Factions have no special mechanics to interact with each other.

They interact through play, economics, violence, and Britain's reaction.

That's intentional.

Why This Is the Right Call

If you add:

  • Faction treaties
  • Faction recognition layers
  • Faction courts
  • Faction arbitration

…you accidentally recreate a parallel civic system, which contradicts your core thesis.

Core Design Principle

You want:

  • One source of law
  • Many sources of power

So faction–faction interaction must be raw, local, and consequence-driven, not formalized.

How Factions Actually Interact (In Practice)

1️⃣ Territorial Conflict (The Primary Mode)

Factions interact first and foremost through space.

  • Raids
  • Sieges
  • Ambushes
  • Occupation attempts
  • Harassment of vendors or supply lines

None of this needs a system.

The only mechanic involved is:

  • Upkeep
  • Death
  • Loss of control

If a faction can't defend or afford its land, it loses it.

That's the interaction.

2️⃣ Economic Pressure

Faction A can hurt Faction B by:

  • Undercutting vendors
  • Blocking trade routes
  • Forcing higher security costs
  • Targeting crafters or suppliers
  • Driving up upkeep indirectly through conflict

There is no "economic warfare button."
There is only making existence more expensive.

Gold is the battlefield.

3️⃣ Informal Alliances (Social, Not Mechanical)

Factions can:

  • Coordinate attacks
  • Share intelligence
  • Temporarily cooperate
  • Betray each other instantly

There is:

  • No alliance flag
  • No shared chat
  • No auto-defense

If they stop helping, nothing breaks.

This keeps alliances fragile and real.

4️⃣ Britain as the Consequence Amplifier

This is the critical layer.

Faction–faction conflict matters when Britain reacts.

Examples:

  • Attacking a recognized faction risks outlawry
  • Harboring an outlawed faction risks sanctions
  • Repeated aggression may cause Britain to:
    • Withdraw recognition
    • Refuse trade
    • Justify bounties
    • Endorse retaliation

Britain doesn't mediate.
Britain reframes.

Faction A doesn't need permission to attack Faction B.
But Britain decides what that attack means.

5️⃣ Legitimacy Warfare (Soft Power)

Factions fight over:

  • Being recognized
  • Staying recognized
  • Getting rivals outlawed

So faction interaction often looks like:

"We don't need to destroy them — we just need Britain to stop recognizing them."

This turns the following into faction weapons:

  • Assassination
  • False-flag attacks
  • Evidence planting
  • Narrative control

Again: no system required.
Only visibility.

6️⃣ Outlaw vs Outlaw Dynamics

Outlawed factions are the wild west.

  • No legal protection
  • No appeal to Britain
  • Violence is expected
  • Reputation matters more than law

This creates:

  • Pirate politics
  • Criminal syndicate rivalries
  • Monster factions fighting over territory

Britain doesn't care unless it spills inward.

What You Explicitly Do Not Need

You do not need:

  • ❌ Faction treaties
  • ❌ Faction fealty
  • ❌ Faction recognition of other factions
  • ❌ Faction courts
  • ❌ Faction arbitration
  • ❌ Faction diplomacy UI

All of that would be fake structure.

The Clean Rule

Lock This In

Factions interact with each other directly.
Britain interacts with factions politically.
There is no layer above that.

Or even cleaner:

Factions fight.
Britain remembers.

Why This Produces Better Gameplay

  • ✅ Alliances are real because they're fragile
  • ✅ Betrayal is easy and meaningful
  • ✅ Power comes from coordination, not UI
  • ✅ Britain stays central without micromanaging
  • ✅ Players create politics instead of navigating menus

This is how UO should feel.

Final Sanity Check

Your World Now Has:

  • One legal authority (Britain)
  • Many territorial powers (factions)
  • One universal currency of survival (gold)
  • One universal currency of legitimacy (recognition)

Faction–faction interaction doesn't need mechanics
because Britain is the mechanic.

Summary

Factions do not have a bespoke "faction diplomacy system." They interact with each other directly through territorial conflict, economic pressure, informal alliances, and violence. The consequence layer comes from Britain's reaction — recognition, toleration, or outlawry — which reframes what faction actions mean politically.

There are no faction treaties, courts, or formal diplomatic mechanics. This is intentional. Britain is the sole source of law. Factions are sources of power. Adding parallel civic structures would contradict the core design.

Factions fight. Britain remembers.
