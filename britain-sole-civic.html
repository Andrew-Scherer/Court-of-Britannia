⚖️ Britain as the Sole Civic System

The Sole Civic Authority in a Faction-Driven Realm

Britain is the only place where law exists.
Factions are the only things that hold land.
Gold determines survival. Recognition determines legitimacy.

Core Thesis

Britain is the only civic institution in the world.

Everything else—cities, ruins, forts, coves, strongholds—is territorial, not civic. Those places are controlled by factions, not governments.

There is no network of city-states.
There is no shared civic layer.

There is Britain…and the world beyond it.

Britain's Role

Britain is the only place where formal law exists.

It is the sole source of:

  • Citizenship / Residency
  • Courts and legal trials
  • Exile and pardon
  • Civic taxation
  • Salaried public offices
  • Official warrants and decrees

Britain never deactivates.
Britain cannot collapse mechanically.

Britain is not powerful because it controls land.
Britain is powerful because it controls legitimacy.

The World Beyond Britain

Outside Britain, there are no cities in the civic sense.

There are:

  • Faction-controlled territories
  • Pre-placed structures and landmarks
  • Economically maintained strongholds
  • Contested regions without formal law

Territorial Control Logic

A place exists politically only if a faction can afford to hold it.

If upkeep is paid:

  • The faction controls the space
  • Vendors operate
  • NPCs align
  • Authority exists

If upkeep fails:

  • Control collapses
  • Buildings open
  • Vendors vanish
  • Power dissolves

There is no population requirement.
There is no civic activation.
Only economic viability.

Factions as Territorial Powers

Factions are the primary world actors.

They:

  • Control land
  • Maintain structures
  • Enforce their own internal rules
  • Rise and fall based on gold flow

Faction authority is real, but not legal by default.

A faction can dominate territory without ever being legitimate.

That distinction matters.

Recognition: Britain's Primary Lever

Britain does not rule factions.
Britain recognizes them—or refuses to.

Recognition is Britain's only outward-facing power.

Recognition States

A faction exists in one of three states relative to Britain:

1. Recognized

Britain treats the faction as a legitimate authority

  • Trade is lawful
  • Warrants are honored
  • Fealty and taxation may apply
  • Violence against them is politically constrained

2. Tolerated

Britain neither recognizes nor suppresses

  • No tribute
  • No protection
  • Gray-zone legitimacy
  • Smugglers, mercenaries, radicals thrive here

3. Outlawed

Britain declares the faction illegitimate

  • Warrants invalid
  • Trade restricted or sanctioned
  • Violence against them is justified politically
  • The faction still exists—just outside the law
Recognition can be granted, withdrawn, or challenged.
It is never permanent.

Fealty: Britain & Factions

Fealty exists only between Britain and factions.

A faction may swear fealty to Britain.

Fealty means:

  • Automatic tribute from faction treasury
  • Public alignment with Britain
  • Expectation (not guarantee) of protection
  • Legitimacy in the eyes of the realm

Fealty is:

  • Voluntary to enter
  • Costly to leave
  • Public
  • Breakable
Britain does not appoint faction leaders.
Britain does not control faction land.

Britain extracts gold and grants legitimacy.

Residency — Britain Only

Residency survives, but only here.

Residency as legal standing in Britain:

  • Residency = citizenship in Britain
  • Exile = loss of standing in Britain
  • Residency ends on permadeath

There is no residency system for factions or territories beyond Britain.

Gold Is Power

There is only one universal activation check:

Can you pay?
  • Faction upkeep determines territorial survival
  • Tribute drains faction resources
  • Overreach kills empires
  • Collapse is automatic and visible

There are no admin switches.
No population math.
No artificial timers.

Gold flow replaces bureaucracy.

Law vs Territory

This distinction is absolute:

Concept Britain The World
Law ✅ Exists ❌ None
Courts ✅ Yes ❌ No
Citizenship ✅ Yes ❌ No
Exile ✅ Yes ❌ No
Territory Control ❌ No ✅ Yes
Upkeep-Based Power ❌ No ✅ Yes
Britain governs people.
Factions govern places.

Violence & Legitimacy

Britain does not prevent violence.
It defines whether violence is justified.

  • Killing a recognized faction leader is an act of war
  • Killing an outlawed leader is enforcement
  • Attacking tolerated factions is politically ambiguous

The system does not judge morality.
Players do.

Outlawry Instead of Rebellion

Britain does not "fight rebels."

Britain withdraws recognition.

Outlawed factions:

  • Still exist
  • Still control territory
  • Are illegitimate
  • Violence against them becomes justified politically

However:

It is quite possible that a rebellious faction, if they grow strong enough, can take control of Britain through brute strength.

If the King dies and a rebel faction takes control of the coronation ceremony, if timing and execution work in their favor, a surviving member of their faction can crown themselves King and completely take over the administrative function of Britain.

Historical Context: William the Conqueror (1066)

  1. William invades England (technically illegal)
  2. He wins one decisive battle
  3. He gets crowned King

After the coronation:

  • All remaining English resistance is declared outlawry
  • Those still fighting are not "defenders" — they are criminals

The Political Ledger

All recognition, fealty, tribute, and outlawry is recorded publicly.

The ledger:

  • Enforces nothing
  • Hides nothing
  • Forgets nothing
History is the punishment.

Design Canon

These rules are absolute:

  1. Britain is the only civic authority
  2. Factions are the only territorial powers
  3. Gold determines survival
  4. Recognition determines legitimacy
  5. Nothing is enforced—everything is visible

There is no empire mechanic.
There is no reset button.

Only choices, pressure, and consequences.

Britain does not rule the world.

It decides who counts.
