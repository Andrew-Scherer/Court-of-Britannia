In a world of **permadeath**, succession is never guaranteed. A King names multiple heirs for strategic reasons:

### 4. Hedge Against Betrayal

## ðŸ¤ Why a King Appoints Heirs

A wise King appoints heirs for strategic reasons:

### 1. Ensure Smooth Succession

### 2. Legitimize the Next Ruler

### 3. Control Succession

Appointing heirs ensures the Crown passes to someone the King trusts â€” whether by blood, merit, or loyalty. Multiple heirs allow the King to balance different factions.

### 4. Prevent Power Vacuums

Heirs act as backup rulers if the King dies unexpectedly, reducing the risk of long-term [[Interregnum|Succession]]. Multiple heirs mean multiple safeguards against total succession collapse.

### 5. Political Signaling

Appointing heirs sends a message to the realm about who the King favors, creating alliances and deterring rivals. The **order of precedence** signals the King's priorities and strategic alignments.

### 6. Dynasty Insurance (Permadeath)

In a permadeath environment, no successor is guaranteed to survive. Multiple heirs are **survival insurance** â€” if one dies, another carries the dynasty forward. This is not paranoia, it's pragmatism.
