üèõÔ∏è Land Commissioner

Role Summary

The Land Commissioner is the senior executor of the King's land authority. A Steward with personnel control and extra social weight. This is a hands-on role, not manager-only.

Powers & Responsibilities

What Land Commissioners Can Do:

  • Execute evictions physically on properties
  • Place eviction notices and property claims
  • Deed properties to new owners
  • Act on Royal Orders from the King
  • Act without Royal Orders (at significant risk)
  • Hire and fire individual Stewards
  • Suspend Stewards immediately for misconduct
  • Manage Land Ledger and property records
  • Investigate land disputes and irregularities

Authority & Limits

Authority Over:

  • Royal Property ‚Äî Only with King's explicit order
  • Non-Royal Property ‚Äî Direct authority to manage without royal approval
  • Personnel ‚Äî Full hiring/firing authority over Stewards
  • Land Ledger ‚Äî Maintain and review all land action logs
Cannot:
  • Override the King's legitimacy on Royal Property
  • Act on Royal Property without consequences if unauthorized
  • Protect Stewards (or themselves) from fallout of unauthorized actions

Appointment & Dismissal

Appointed By: The King directly

Term: At the King's pleasure

Removal: King can dismiss at any time

Critical: Former Land Commissioners remain accountable for all actions taken while in office. Dismissal does NOT grant immunity from prosecution, exile, or execution.

Why This Role Matters

This role is desirable because:

  • Control over the most visible resource (buildings)
  • Ability to enrich allies and destroy enemies
  • Personnel authority creates a power base
  • Every action creates political leverage
  • Dangerous enough to matter, visible enough to build reputation

‚öñÔ∏è Steward

Role Summary

Stewards are distributed executors with real mechanical power over land. Expendable hands who can deed property, execute evictions, and place notices. Visible, fallible, corruptible, and fully accountable.

Powers & Responsibilities

What Stewards Can Do:

  • Execute evictions physically on properties
  • Place eviction notices and property claims
  • Deed properties to new owners
  • Act with or without authorization (at risk)
  • Record land actions in the Land Ledger
  • Serve as first point of information for property questions

Authority & Limits

Authority Over:

  • Non-Royal Property ‚Äî Can act independently
  • Royal Property ‚Äî Can execute King's orders (or act without them at extreme risk)
  • Land Records ‚Äî Maintain accurate logging
Cannot:
  • Hire or fire other Stewards
  • Protect themselves from consequences of unauthorized actions
  • Override decisions of the Land Commissioner

Can be suspended instantly by the Land Commissioner.

Appointment & Dismissal

Appointed By: Land Commissioner

Term: At Land Commissioner's discretion

Removal: Land Commissioner can dismiss or suspend immediately

Critical: Former Stewards remain accountable for all actions taken while in office. Dismissal does NOT grant immunity from prosecution, exile, or execution.

Why This Role Matters

This role is desirable because:

  • Real power without top-level scrutiny
  • Ability to help friends, harm enemies
  • Opportunities for corruption and favors
  • Every action is visible and creates consequences
  • Dangerous, which makes it exciting

Requirements & How to Join

Becoming a Steward

  1. Petition the Land Commissioner ‚Äî Request an interview
  2. Evaluation ‚Äî Commissioner assesses trustworthiness and competence
  3. Appointment ‚Äî Commissioner grants Steward status (or denies)
  4. Immediate Powers ‚Äî Gain mechanical land powers as soon as appointed
  5. All Actions Logged ‚Äî From moment of appointment, everything you do is recorded

Becoming Land Commissioner

  1. King's Direct Appointment ‚Äî No petition process
  2. Trust & Politics ‚Äî King chooses based on loyalty, competence, politics

Protections & Accountability

What Protects You

  • Acting with proper authorization (King's order for Royal Property, Commissioner's direction for Stewards)
  • Accurate record-keeping in the Land Ledger
  • Staying within your authority (Non-Royal Property for routine actions)
  • Political support from the King or powerful figures

What Exposes You

  • Acting on Royal Property without King's order
  • Corrupt deeding (giving property to friends when it's not OPEN)
  • Unauthorized evictions without proper cause
  • Abuse of power for personal gain
  • Making enemies with Magistrates, nobles, or the King

Safeguards Against Abuse

The system includes protections to prevent complete chaos while preserving political risk:

  1. Full Action Logging ‚Äî Every deed, eviction, and notice is recorded with actor, timestamp, and reason
  2. Instant Suspension ‚Äî Land Commissioner can suspend a Steward immediately, removing their powers
  3. 24-Hour Challenge Window ‚Äî King or Magistrate can cancel unauthorized actions within 24 hours
  4. No Immunity Upon Dismissal ‚Äî Former Stewards/Commissioners can be prosecuted for past actions

Integration with Other Systems

Chronicle

All Land Ledger entries are exported to Chronicle. Scandals around unauthorized actions become public record. Trials reference the Ledger as evidence.

Exile

Exiled individuals cannot receive property deeds. Stewards attempting to deed to exiles face prosecution. Commissioner can suspend Stewards who violate exile orders.

Permadeath

When a property holder dies, automated grace period begins. Stewards deed to new claimants after grace period expires. For Royal Property, King orders new grant.
