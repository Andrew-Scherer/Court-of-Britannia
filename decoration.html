Unrestricted and De-Monetized

The Core Principle

Decoration is not progression.
It is language.
We give everyone the alphabet.

What "Unrestricted" Means

Players have free access to decorative items.

Including items often paywalled on other shards:

  • Spider webs
  • Blood splatters
  • Candles and torches
  • Banners and flags
  • Bones and skulls
  • Clutter and ambiance props
  • Furniture
  • Environmental effects

Decoration Is Part of Expression, Not Progression

Decorations are not:

  • A reward
  • A prestige gate
  • A monetization layer
  • A status symbol

Decorations are tools for expression.

Why This Is Monumental

1. It Removes "Aesthetic Power" From the Economy

On many shards:

  • Rare decorations = status
  • Status = soft power
  • Soft power = influence

That creates:

  • Haves vs have-nots
  • Cosmetic elitism
  • Pay-to-feel-important dynamics

By making decorations free:

You collapse aesthetic hierarchy entirely.

Power returns to:

  • Presence
  • Recognition
  • Territory
  • Reputation

Exactly where you want it.

2. It Prevents Monetization From Undermining Politics

This is subtle but crucial.

If decorations are monetized:

  • The richest players visually dominate
  • Political centers look purchased, not earned
  • Legitimacy becomes confused with spending

Free decoration ensures:

  • Visual identity reflects play, not wallets
  • Faction spaces feel authored, not bought
  • Immersion stays intact
  • Your world remains diegetic

3. It Encourages Shared Spaces to Feel Alive

When decorations are free:

  • Players experiment
  • Spaces change
  • Buildings feel lived-in
  • Hubs become social magnets

If decorations are scarce or monetized:

  • Players hoard them
  • Spaces stay empty
  • Expression is cautious
Free expression = vibrant world.

4. It Aligns Perfectly With Removing In-House Storage

This pairing is important.

You removed:

  • Functional power from housing

So you must increase:

  • Expressive freedom

Otherwise housing feels sterile.

This Restores The Balance

No power + Lots of personality

Housing becomes about identity, not accumulation.

What This Does NOT Break

Free decorations do not:

  • Reduce conflict
  • Trivialize loss
  • Create safety
  • Undermine risk
  • Affect economy meaningfully

Because:

  • Decorations vanish when property is lost
  • They don't store value
  • They don't transfer power
Loss still matters.
It just doesn't bankrupt creativity.

The Sacred Line

If It Affects Power, It's Not Decoration

This line must be sacred.

Decorations are:

  • Visual
  • Aesthetic
  • Atmospheric
  • Expressive

Decorations are NOT:

  • Functional
  • Combat-affecting
  • Economy-altering
  • Power-granting

Free access doesn't change this line.
It reinforces it.

How to Frame This

Some players might worry this is "free stuff" that devalues effort.

The response:

This is not "everyone gets everything."

This is "everyone gets tools to express themselves."

Decoration is not progression.
It is language.
We give everyone the alphabet.

That reframes it instantly.

Examples of What's Free

Ambiance Items

  • Candles, torches, braziers
  • Spider webs and cobwebs
  • Blood stains and splatters
  • Bones, skulls, remains
  • Clutter, debris, props

Identity Items

  • Banners and flags
  • Signs and labels
  • Faction symbols
  • Heraldry components

Furniture

  • Tables, chairs, benches
  • Beds, bedrolls
  • Shelves, crates, barrels
  • Rugs, curtains

Atmosphere

  • Plants and vegetation
  • Statues and monuments
  • Water features
  • Lighting effects

Decorations Are Temporary

When a property is lost:

  • All decorations vanish
  • The space reverts to default
  • Expression is cleared

This is intentional.

Because decorations are free:

  • Losing them stings emotionally
  • But doesn't bankrupt you economically
  • You can redecorate a new space instantly
  • Recovery is fast
Decorations mark current occupation,
not permanent investment.
