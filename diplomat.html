What Diplomats Do

Diplomats conduct the daily work of Britain's external presence. They deliver messages and proclamations, observe and report on faction activities, attend faction events as Britain's representatives, and create the initial contact points between Britain and factions.

Diplomats operate in spaces where Guards have no authority and Military presence would escalate. They are the visible face of Britain in faction territories, relying on diplomatic protection and Britain's reputation to stay safe.

They are not powerful. They are dangerous to touch.

Diplomatic Protection

All Diplomats operate under diplomatic protection‚Äîa political norm that makes harming them an attack on Britain itself.

Harming a Diplomat is never "just PvP." It is a political act Britain must respond to.

Why Protection Exists

  • Britain is powerful enough to retaliate
  • Factions value future negotiations
  • Communication channels remaining open benefits all parties
  • Attacking diplomats creates casus belli

When Protection Breaks Down

  • Britain is weak or distracted
  • The faction sees no value in future dialogue
  • The diplomat is caught spying or inciting rebellion
  • During total war, when norms collapse

Attacking a Diplomat creates the same consequence as attacking the High Emissary or Envoy: casus belli. Britain does not distinguish between ranks when diplomatic protection is violated‚Äîall three carry Britain's authority equally in the field.

Operating in Faction Territories

No Mechanical Authority

Diplomats have no enforcement powers in faction spaces:

  • Cannot make arrests
  • Cannot use force
  • Cannot seize property
  • Cannot command Guards or Military outside Britain

Their authority is political, not mechanical. Factions listen because Britain can retaliate if they don't‚Äîor they ignore diplomats when Britain cannot.

Reliance on Presence and Witness

Diplomats create consequences through visibility:

  • "We delivered the warning" ‚Äî Removes "we didn't know" as a defense
  • "They refused publicly" ‚Äî Creates documented justification for escalation
  • "We witnessed the oath" ‚Äî Binds factions to their word
  • "They attacked a diplomat" ‚Äî Justifies Britain's response

Relationship With Other Roles

Envoys

Diplomats report to Envoys who:

  • Assign specific missions and territories
  • Provide direction on proclamations to deliver
  • Receive intelligence reports from the field
  • Appoint and dismiss Diplomats

High Emissary

The High Emissary sets overall diplomatic strategy. While Diplomats rarely interact directly with the High Emissary, their work implements the High Emissary's vision for Britain's external relationships.

Guards and Military

Diplomats coordinate with Guards and Military when:

  • Tax enforcement escalates ‚Äî Guards provide backing for seizures
  • Escort is needed ‚Äî Military protects diplomats traveling through hostile territories
  • Intelligence is critical ‚Äî Diplomats warn of imminent attacks

Tension arises when:

  • Diplomats claim more time will resolve a crisis; Military sees delay as weakness
  • Military action undermines ongoing diplomatic efforts
  • Diplomats demand restraint; Military demands freedom of action

Political Temptations and Risks

Developing Faction Loyalties

Diplomats assigned to specific factions over time may develop loyalty to those factions over Britain:

  • Personal relationships with faction leaders
  • Respect for faction culture and values
  • Belief that Britain's policy toward the faction is unjust
  • Offers of better treatment from powerful factions
Diplomatic Defection is Subtle

Unlike military coups, diplomatic defection operates through:

  • Selective reporting (suppressing unfavorable intelligence)
  • Delayed deliveries (buying factions time to prepare)
  • Sympathetic interpretation (presenting faction positions favorably)
  • Warning factions of Britain's intentions

Intelligence Manipulation

Diplomats who suppress or fabricate intelligence can:

  • Engineer crises by hiding warnings
  • Blind Britain to faction threats
  • Create false justifications for war
  • Favor one claimant during succession crises

By the time defection is obvious, Britain's external relationships have already shifted.

Succession Crises

When Britain's authority fragments, Diplomats face the same choice as Guards and Military: Which claimant do we recognize?

Diplomatic recognition in faction territories is constitutive‚Äîit creates external legitimacy:

  • If Diplomats recognize Claimant A ‚Äî Factions see A as Britain's legitimate ruler
  • If Diplomats recognize Claimant B ‚Äî Factions shift allegiance accordingly
  • If Diplomats split recognition ‚Äî Factions exploit the division; succession crisis prolongs

Why Play This Role?

Diplomats are for players who want:

  • Access to faction territories ‚Äî Explore beyond Britain's walls
  • Intelligence networks ‚Äî Learn faction secrets and plans
  • Political leverage ‚Äî Influence conflicts before they begin
  • Diplomatic protection ‚Äî Make violence against you politically expensive
  • Entry to the diplomatic corps ‚Äî Path to becoming Envoy or High Emissary
  • Temptation ‚Äî The slow realization you could serve factions over the Crown

This is the entry-level diplomatic role with significant autonomy and political impact.
