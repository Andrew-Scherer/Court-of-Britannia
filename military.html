Authority Through Force and Projection

What the Military Is

Britain's Military is not law enforcement and not governance. It is the Crown's capacity to project violence beyond the city walls and to survive environments where law does not function.

Where Guards make authority visible, the Military makes authority possible.

They exist to:

  • Fight where arrest is meaningless
  • Hold territory through presence
  • Deter threats before they reach Britain
  • Remind everyone that the city is not defenseless

Like the Guards, the Military serves authority as it exists in practice, not theory.

Guards vs Military: Critical Distinction

City Guards

  • Law enforcement inside Britain
  • Answer to courts and magistrates
  • Duties: arrest, patrol, jail
  • Not expeditionary

Military / Expendables

  • External force projection
  • Used against outlaw factions, in protectorates, as occupation presence
  • Not bound by civilian law the same way
  • Lethal force is default, not exception
Can one player be both? Yes ‚Äî but not at the same time. Think in terms of posting, not identity. A player may be a Guard while stationed in Britain, then reassigned to Military duty externally. Switching roles is visible and political. This creates: "The Guard has been militarized," "Britain is escalating," "This isn't policing anymore." That's good tension.

Why Play This?

If Guards are about:

  • Order
  • Restraint
  • Legality
  • Public legitimacy

The Military is about:

  • Survival
  • Force
  • Initiative
  • Political leverage

Military players get:

  • External campaigns
  • Patrol authority outside the city
  • Command of armed groups
  • Strategic power
  • And the long-term temptation to stop obeying civilians

This is the role for players who want power that isn't clean.

Philosophy

Guards ask: "Is this lawful?"
Military asks: "Will this hold?"

The Military assumes:

  • Violence is expected
  • Enemies won't submit to arrest
  • Legitimacy comes after control
  • Hesitation kills

This difference is why Guards and Military cooperate‚Äîand why they mistrust each other.

Authority and Allegiance

Military authority flows through appointment, just like the Guards‚Äîbut from a parallel chain, not the same one.

Sources of authority:

  • The Crown ‚Äî Ultimate legitimacy
  • The High Commander ‚Äî Operational control
  • Field Commanders ‚Äî Local authority
  • Officers ‚Äî Unit-level leadership
The Military does not answer to:
  • Magistrates
  • Courts
  • Wardens
  • City law

That exemption is intentional and dangerous.

High Commander of Britain

The High Commander controls Britain's external violence.

They:

  • Decide where forces are deployed
  • Define rules of engagement
  • Command campaigns and protectorates
  • Control fortifications outside the city
  • Determine when military force replaces diplomacy
This is the second most dangerous office in Britain.

A loyal High Commander protects the realm. An ambitious one doesn't need permission.

Historically, Kings fall not when armies lose‚Äîbut when they stop obeying.

Field Commanders

Field Commanders control:

  • Regions
  • Borders
  • Protectorates
  • Major external threats

They act with High Commander authority when distant from Britain and must make decisions without civilian oversight.

Field Commanders build:

  • Personal loyalty networks
  • Hardened veterans
  • Regional power bases

They are the most common source of military coups.

Soldiers

Soldiers are:

  • Professional fighters
  • Trained for lethal combat
  • Accustomed to loss and brutality
  • Loyal to officers before ideals

They are not peacekeepers. They are not guards. They are force applied until resistance ends.

Core Duties

The Military is responsible for:

  • External patrols beyond city walls
  • Escorting caravans and nobles through hostile lands
  • Holding protectorates and occupied territories
  • Responding to large-scale threats (raids, invasions, monsters)
  • Conducting punitive expeditions
  • Establishing forward camps and forts
  • Supporting Guards during internal emergencies (by request or decree)

Use of Force

Lethal Force (Standard)

Unlike Guards, lethal force is the default.

The Military:

  • Kills enemies
  • Breaks resistance
  • Does not attempt arrest unless ordered
  • Does not preserve enemies for trial

Mercy is strategic, not procedural.

Relationship With the Guards

Fireman vs Police

Normal State: Cooperation

  • Guards maintain internal order
  • Military handles external threats
  • Guards request military aid during crises
  • Military defers to Guards inside Britain

Tension State: Jurisdictional Conflict

  • Military claims threat justifies action inside city
  • Guards insist on law and restraint
  • Arrest vs execution disputes
  • Chain-of-command ambiguity

Crisis State: Alignment or Schism

If:

  • Marshal and High Commander cooperate ‚Üí Britain becomes authoritarian
  • Marshal and High Commander oppose ‚Üí Britain fractures
  • Military bypasses Guards ‚Üí Soft coup
  • Guards block Military ‚Üí Civil conflict risk

This is intentional.

Political Ambition & Risk (Critical)

Like the Guards, the Military is structurally capable of takeover.

Danger Signs

  • Soldiers loyal to commanders, not Crown
  • Military acting without Guard coordination
  • Forts controlling supply routes
  • Field Commanders ignoring recalls
  • Guards "requesting" military presence inside city
A High Commander aligned with a Marshal can:
  • Suspend courts
  • Control access
  • Neutralize rivals
  • Rule "temporarily" for stability

History says temporary never ends.

Summary

  • Britain's Military is a parallel power structure to the Guards, focused on external force and survival
  • Authority flows through a separate chain, creating cooperation and rivalry
  • Lethal force is standard; law is secondary
  • High-ranking military nobles can become powerful political actors
  • Alignment between Military and Guards creates authoritarian potential
  • Conflict between them signals regime instability
  • The Military exists to make protection, occupation, and escalation visible
  • Field Commanders are the most common source of military coups
  • A High Commander aligned with a Marshal represents existential threat to civilian rule

Final Truth

You didn't want "soldiers." You wanted a second sword, a second loyalty network, a second path to tyranny. You now have it.
