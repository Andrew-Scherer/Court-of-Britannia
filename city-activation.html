Population-Scaled Governance

Political systems activate through residency, not presence.
Power follows population. Absence dissolves authority.

Core Principle

Cities are not always "on." A city only becomes politically and economically active when enough players choose to live there.

Until then, it is:

  • Administratively dormant
  • Economically inert
  • Politically silent

This ensures Britain remains the gravitational core until the world can sustain fragmentation.

Automatic Activation

No human toggles. No admin switches. No staff intervention.

When Resident Count Crosses Upward Threshold

  1. System detects threshold crossing automatically
  2. City state changes immediately
  3. Relevant systems engage
  4. Public announcement fires
  5. Ledgers begin recording

This happens:

  • When enough new residents declare
  • When residents return from other cities
  • When exile is lifted and residents rejoin
Core Rule: Activation is automatic. Cities "wake up" when people choose them.

Automatic Deactivation

Cities can fall back into dormancy if population drops.

When Resident Count Crosses Downward Threshold

If a city falls below its threshold, deactivation is not immediate. There is a grace period to prevent oscillation.

Deactivation Grace Period: 7 Days

When a city drops below its threshold:

  • A warning is published publicly
  • Systems remain active for 7 real days
  • If population does not recover, deactivation begins
  • If population recovers, grace period cancels

Dormant Wind-Down Process

When grace period expires without recovery:

  1. Timers Resolve ‚Äî Jail custody, auctions, pending decrees all resolve immediately
  2. Salaries Stop ‚Äî No new salary payments issued
  3. Taxes Cease ‚Äî Local taxes no longer collected
  4. Fealty Treaties Suspend ‚Äî Tribute stops flowing, protection promises pause
  5. Ledgers Freeze ‚Äî Become read-only (historical record preserved)
  6. Roles Vacate ‚Äî Government offices become vacant
  7. Treasury Locks ‚Äî Balance preserved but inaccessible

After wind-down completes:

  • City enters Dormant state
  • Residents may remain (they become politically unprotected)
  • Geography and buildings persist
  • History is preserved in frozen ledgers
Why Wind-Down Matters: This makes decline visible and political, not abrupt. Players can see a city dying and respond before it's too late.

Britain's Special Status

Britain is always active, regardless of population.

Why Britain Is Different

  • Britain is the anchor ‚Äî The default, the womb, the center of the world
  • Britain cannot deactivate ‚Äî Even if population collapses to zero
  • Systems always run ‚Äî Treasury, taxes, salaries continue
  • New characters spawn here by default ‚Äî When no other Capital exists

However:

  • Britain can lose Capital status if another city crosses 40+ residents
  • Britain can become politically hollow (active but empty)
  • Britain can become subordinate to another city (through fealty)
Core Principle: Britain is immortal as a system, but not as a power.

Hysteresis Buffer (Anti-Flapping)

To prevent rapid state changes, thresholds have different values for activation vs retention.

State Activate At Retain Until Below
Settled 10 residents 8 residents
Active 25 residents 22 residents
Capital-Eligible 40 residents 35 residents

Example:

  • City activates at 25 residents
  • If population drops to 24, city remains active (grace period begins)
  • Only if population drops below 22 does deactivation trigger

This prevents "flickering" where a city activates and deactivates repeatedly.

Fealty During Dormancy

What happens to fealty relationships when a city deactivates?

If a Vassal City Deactivates

  • Fealty treaty suspends automatically
  • Tribute payments cease
  • Protection obligations pause
  • Treaty remains in records but is inactive
  • If city reactivates, treaty resumes unless revoked

If a Liege City Deactivates

  • Vassal cities become automatically neutral
  • Tribute obligations cease
  • Protection promises void
  • Vassals may seek new liege or remain independent
Political Consequence: A capital that loses population loses its vassals. This creates cascading collapse.

Migration Pressure and Activation

Activation thresholds create natural migration dynamics.

Scenario: Players Want to Build a New City

  1. 10 players coordinate
  2. All declare residency in Yew simultaneously
  3. Yew crosses into Settled status
  4. They attract 15 more residents
  5. Yew activates as full city-state
  6. Internal politics begin
  7. Britain loses 25 residents (political crisis)

Result: Player-driven city founding becomes content, not admin event.

Scenario: City Loses Legitimacy

  1. Unpopular regime angers residents
  2. Residents leave gradually
  3. City drops below 25 residents
  4. Grace period warning fires
  5. Regime can respond (reform, pardon exiles, lower taxes)
  6. Or ignore and face deactivation

Result: Population becomes leverage. Residents can "vote with their feet."

Common Questions

Q: Can a city reactivate after going dormant?

A: Yes. If residents return and cross the threshold, the city wakes up. Previous history remains in frozen ledgers.

Q: What happens to my property if the city deactivates?

A: Property remains. You still own it. But if the city stays dormant long enough, it may become subject to decay or reassignment (see Property System).

Q: Can guards from a defunct city still arrest me?

A: No. When a city deactivates, its roles vacate. Former guards have no authority.

Q: If Britain loses population, do systems shut down?

A: No. Britain is always active. But political legitimacy erodes if population drops significantly.

Q: Can I form a city with 9 friends?

A: Not yet. You need 10 to reach Settled, and 25 for full activation. This is intentional ‚Äî prevents premature fragmentation.

Q: What if everyone leaves Britain?

A: Britain remains active systemically, but becomes politically hollow. New players still spawn there by default. This creates "ghost capital" RP ‚Äî very interesting.

Design Intent

This system ensures:

  • Low populations stay dense ‚Äî No premature fragmentation
  • Growth is player-driven ‚Äî Cities "earn" activation through migration
  • Decline is visible ‚Äî Grace periods make collapse dramatic
  • No admin babysitting ‚Äî Everything automatic
  • Population becomes political ‚Äî Residents are leverage, not scenery
