The Foundation of Political Authority

A city exists when people choose it.
Until then, it is only stone.

Core Definition

Residency is the formal declaration of civic membership. It is not presence. It is not combat flagging. It is not ownership.

Residency is a political commitment that answers the question: "Which city do you belong to?"

Everything else follows from this choice:

  • Which taxes you pay
  • Which laws bind you
  • Which courts judge you
  • Which ledgers track you
  • Which city counts you toward activation thresholds

The Residency Ledger

Every city maintains a public Residency Ledger ‚Äî a physical object (stone, book, monument) that records all declared residents.

What the Ledger Contains

Field Purpose
Resident Name Character who declared residency
Date Declared When residency began
Official Role (if any) King, Guard, Magistrate, etc.
Online Status Currently logged in (yes/no)
Active/Exiled Current standing

The ledger is publicly accessible. Anyone can see who lives where and whether they hold office.

This visibility serves multiple purposes:

  • Players know who they can contact for RP
  • Cities can verify population counts
  • Exile enforcement becomes visible
  • Migration patterns become narratively significant

How to Declare Residency

To become a resident of a city, a character must:

  1. Travel to the city physically
  2. Interact with the city's Residency Ledger
  3. Declare residency through a confirmation gump
  4. Accept the city's jurisdiction

Declaration Is Immediate

Upon confirmation:

  • Previous residency is automatically revoked
  • New city's tax system applies immediately
  • Previous city's resident count decreases
  • New city's resident count increases
  • Change is logged publicly

Example Declaration Text

You are about to declare residency in the City of Yew. This will: - End your residency in Britain - Subject you to Yew's laws and taxes - Count you toward Yew's population threshold - Make you ineligible for Britain-specific offices - Apply a 24-hour cooldown before you can change residency again Do you wish to proceed? [Declare Residency] [Cancel]

How to Change Residency

Residency is not permanent, but changing it is not casual.

The 24-Hour Cooldown

After declaring residency in any city, you cannot change residency again for 24 hours.

Why this matters:

  • Prevents population count manipulation
  • Makes migration a commitment
  • Prevents spy accounts from flipping rapidly
  • Adds weight to "exodus" events
The cooldown does not apply to:
  • Forced exile (immediate)
  • City deactivation (residents auto-migrate)
  • First-time character residency declaration

Process for Changing Residency

  1. Wait for cooldown to expire (if applicable)
  2. Travel to the new city
  3. Interact with new city's Residency Ledger
  4. Declare residency
  5. Previous city residency revokes automatically

No permission required. No approval needed. This is a player choice.

What Residency Grants

Residency is identity, not privilege.

Residency Does NOT Grant:

  • ‚ùå Free housing
  • ‚ùå Automatic income
  • ‚ùå Combat advantages
  • ‚ùå Access to restricted areas
  • ‚ùå Protection from arrest
  • ‚ùå Immunity to exile

Residency DOES Grant:

  • ‚úÖ Legal standing in that city's courts
  • ‚úÖ Eligibility for city-specific offices
  • ‚úÖ Contribution to city activation thresholds
  • ‚úÖ Inclusion in city census and ledgers
  • ‚úÖ Narrative belonging ("I am of Yew")
Core Principle: Residency is civic membership. It creates standing, not safety.

Death and Residency

This is a permadeath shard. When a character dies permanently, their residency ends automatically.

What Happens on Permadeath

  • Residency is revoked immediately
  • Character is removed from Residency Ledger
  • City resident count decreases
  • Any held offices vacate (Seat System)
  • Property enters succession process (Property System)

There is no "ghost residency." Death severs all civic ties.

Exile, Banishment, and Forced Residency Loss

Residency is normally voluntary. Exile is the exception.

How Exile Works

A character may be exiled by:

  • King's decree
  • Regent's decree (when King is dead/absent)
  • Magistrate's sentence (after trial)

Effect of Exile:

  • Residency is revoked immediately
  • Character is removed from Residency Ledger
  • Character is marked "EXILED" in public records
  • Cannot declare residency in that city again
  • City resident count decreases
Critical Rule: Exile is city-specific. A character exiled from Britain can still be a resident of Yew.

Lifting Exile

Exile can only be lifted by:

  • The ruler who exiled the character (King/Regent)
  • A new ruler (if leadership changes through succession)
  • Death of the exiled character (moot point on permadeath shard)

Political Consequence: When a new King is crowned, they inherit the previous King's exiles. They may choose to pardon or maintain them. This creates immediate political pressure.

Migration and Political Pressure

Residency creates migration as political theater.

Why Players Migrate

  • Lower taxes in another city
  • Political refuge from hostile regime
  • Opportunity for office (new city needs guards, magistrates)
  • Ideological alignment (support for a claimant)
  • Economic opportunity (vacant properties, active trade)
  • Organized exodus ("We're leaving Britain together")

Why Migration Matters

When residents leave a city:

  • Resident count drops
  • Treasury income decreases
  • City may lose activation status
  • Political legitimacy erodes
  • Narrative of decline becomes visible

When residents join a city:

  • Resident count rises
  • City may activate or strengthen
  • New offices become viable
  • Political momentum builds
  • Narrative of ascendancy emerges
Design Victory: Players moving between cities becomes newsworthy. "Twenty residents left Britain for Yew this week" is a political crisis.

Residency and Non-Residents

A character does not need to be a resident to visit a city.

Non-Residents Can:

  • Travel through the city freely
  • Trade with vendors
  • Attend public events
  • Speak in taverns
  • Be arrested if they break local laws

Non-Residents Cannot:

  • Hold office in that city
  • Vote (if voting systems exist)
  • Count toward city activation thresholds
  • Claim "I am a citizen" for legal standing

Exiled characters are a special case: They may physically enter the city, but may be arrested on sight if the city chooses to enforce exile.

Residency vs. Property Ownership

These are separate systems.

Question Answer
Can I own property in a city where I'm not a resident? Yes (subject to property rules and city policies)
If I'm exiled, do I lose my property? Not automatically, but the city may choose to evict you
Can I change residency and keep my property? Yes
Does residency grant me property? No

See: Property & Auctions for ownership mechanics

Common Scenarios

Scenario: Mass Exodus from Britain

Situation: Twenty players declare residency in Yew over three days due to unpopular King

Result:

  • Britain's resident count drops
  • Yew's resident count rises
  • If Yew crosses 25-resident threshold, it activates
  • If Britain drops below stability threshold, political crisis begins
  • Both events are logged and visible

Scenario: Exile During Succession Crisis

Situation: King exiles rival, King dies, Heir becomes new King

Result:

  • Exile persists through succession
  • New King may pardon immediately (political cost)
  • Or maintain exile (signal of continuity)
  • Exiled character may declare residency elsewhere

Scenario: Player Wants to Be "Homeless"

Situation: Player wants no city affiliation

Result:

  • System requires residency in at least one city
  • Player may choose a "default" city (Britain)
  • Or declare residency in smallest/neutral city
  • Truly stateless characters are not mechanically supported
