Justice, Criminals, and Bounty Hunters

Crime has consequences. Justice has a price. Hunters have a career.

Core Principle

The Bounty System creates real consequences for criminal behavior and legitimate careers for those who enforce justice outside the formal guard structure.

Key Features:

  • Bounties issued by Magistrate, King, Queen, or Regent
  • Paid from City Treasury
  • Premium for capturing criminals alive (full price)
  • Half price for bringing them back dead
  • Public bounty board with last known locations
  • Private Bounty Log tracking all actions

Who Can Issue Bounties

Bounty Authority

Bounties can be issued by:

  • Magistrate ‚Äî Through formal court ruling
  • King ‚Äî By royal decree
  • Queen ‚Äî By royal decree
  • Regent ‚Äî By acting authority decree

Methods of Issuing:

  • By Ruling ‚Äî Formal court decision after trial or hearing
  • By Word ‚Äî Direct decree from King, Queen, or Regent

Bounty Payment Structure

Alive vs Dead

Delivery Method Payment Reason
Alive (subdued and dragged to jail) Full bounty price Allows real justice through court of law
Dead (player's head returned) Half bounty price Justice denied, but threat eliminated
Why the Premium for Alive?

Bringing a criminal back alive allows them to face trial, answer for their crimes publicly, and provides the opportunity for exile, sentencing, or other judicial outcomes. This preserves the integrity of the justice system while still rewarding hunters.

Payment Source

All bounties are paid from the City Treasury.

  • No personal bounties (prevents abuse)
  • Official state-sanctioned justice only
  • Bounty hunters work for the crown, not private vendettas

The Two Interfaces

1. Public Bounty Board

What Players See

The public bounty board displays:

  • Target Name ‚Äî Who is wanted
  • Crime ‚Äî What they're accused of
  • Current Reward ‚Äî Bounty amount (alive/dead split shown)
  • Issue Date ‚Äî When bounty was placed
  • Last Seen Location ‚Äî Where target was last spotted
  • Issuing Authority ‚Äî Who ordered the bounty

Threaded Message Board

The bounty board functions as a threaded message system where players can:

  • Leave tips on criminal locations
  • Share sightings and movement patterns
  • Coordinate hunting efforts
  • Warn others of dangerous targets

This creates community engagement and makes hunting a collaborative effort.

2. Private Bounty Log

Administrative Record

Similar to the Land Registry, the Bounty Log tracks:

  • Actor: Who issued/cancelled/claimed the bounty
  • Action: Type (issued, cancelled, claimed, paid)
  • Target: Criminal name
  • Amount: Bounty value
  • Timestamp: When action occurred

Who Can View Bounty Log:

  • Magistrate
  • King
  • Queen
  • Regent
  • Chancellor (treasury oversight)

Bounty Cancellation

Manual Cancellation

Bounties can be cancelled manually by the same authorities who can issue them:

  • Magistrate
  • King
  • Queen
  • Regent

Reasons for Cancellation:

  • Criminal captured and sentenced
  • Crime pardoned
  • New evidence exonerates the accused
  • Political reasons (deals, alliances, etc.)

Automatic Expiration

24-Hour Cancellation Window

Bounties include a 24-hour timer that can cancel the bounty, but it must be manually triggered. This prevents old, irrelevant bounties from cluttering the system while still requiring deliberate action.

Legitimate Roles Created

1. The Criminal

Risk and Consequence

While players can:

  • Rob indiscriminately
  • Steal without mercy
  • Kill without provocation

This doesn't mean they will be able to do so for very long without:

  • Significant punishment
  • Resources set aside to answer for their crimes
  • Constant threat of bounty hunters
  • Limited safe zones

Being a criminal becomes a legitimate playstyle with real stakes.

2. The Bounty Hunter

A Real Career

The bounty system gives players who want to hunt criminals a legitimate profession:

  • Steady income from City Treasury
  • Recognition for successful captures
  • Strategic gameplay (tracking, planning, capturing)
  • Choice between lethal and non-lethal methods
  • Community collaboration via bounty board

How It Works

Step 1: Bounty Issued

  1. Magistrate rules in court OR King/Queen/Regent decrees
  2. Bounty amount set based on crime severity
  3. Target added to public bounty board
  4. Action logged in private Bounty Log
  5. Bounty hunters can now pursue target

Step 2: The Hunt

  1. Hunters review bounty board for targets
  2. Check last known locations and tips from other players
  3. Track target across the world
  4. Decide approach: subdue alive or kill

Step 3: Capture or Kill

Option A: Capture Alive

  1. Hunter subdues target (combat mechanics)
  2. Target is incapacitated but not killed
  3. Hunter drags target to jail
  4. Delivers target to authorities
  5. Receives full bounty payment

Option B: Kill and Return Head

  1. Hunter kills target
  2. Takes target's head as proof
  3. Returns head to authorities
  4. Receives half bounty payment

Step 4: Payment and Resolution

  1. Hunter receives payment from City Treasury
  2. Bounty marked as claimed in Bounty Log
  3. Target removed from public bounty board
  4. If alive: Criminal faces court/sentencing
  5. If dead: Case closed

Integration with Other Systems

Jail System

Bounties delivered alive enter the jail system where they await trial or sentencing. See Jail System for details.

Court System

Magistrate can issue bounties as part of court rulings. Captured criminals face trial unless already convicted.

Chronicle

Major bounty captures become part of the Chronicle, recording who captured whom and under what circumstances.

Permadeath

If a bounty target dies permanently (permadeath), the bounty is automatically cancelled. Hunter receives half payment if they delivered the killing blow.

Example Scenarios

Scenario 1: Clean Capture

  1. Thief steals from Royal Treasury
  2. King issues 3,000 gold bounty
  3. Bounty hunter tracks thief to hideout
  4. Subdues thief without killing
  5. Drags thief to jail
  6. Receives 3,000 gold from City Treasury
  7. Thief faces trial, is sentenced to hard labor

Scenario 2: Lethal Resolution

  1. Murderer kills three citizens
  2. Magistrate issues 10,000 gold bounty
  3. Multiple hunters pursue target
  4. Target resists violently
  5. Hunter kills target in self-defense
  6. Returns head to Magistrate
  7. Receives 5,000 gold (half bounty)
  8. Case closed, threat eliminated

Scenario 3: Bounty Cancelled

  1. Noble accused of treason, bounty issued
  2. New evidence emerges proving innocence
  3. King cancels bounty before capture
  4. Noble exonerated publicly
  5. Bounty removed from board
  6. Cancellation logged in Bounty Log

Preventing Abuse

Safeguards:
  • No Personal Bounties ‚Äî Only official authorities can issue bounties
  • Treasury Payment Only ‚Äî Prevents false bounties with no payment
  • Full Action Logging ‚Äî Every bounty action tracked in Bounty Log
  • Manual Cancellation Required ‚Äî Prevents accidental or automatic removals
  • Authority Accountability ‚Äî Issuing authorities answer for unjust bounties
