Recognition, Legitimacy, and the Power to Define What Counts

The Key Reframe

This Is the Unlock

Factions are already territorial governments.
They just lack automatic legitimacy.

Britain's role is not to rule factions.
Britain's role is to decide which factions are legitimate actors in the realm.

That's it.

Everything else cascades naturally.

Factions already have:

  • Leaders (killable, accountable)
  • Territory
  • Upkeep pressure
  • Collapse mechanics
  • NPC alignment
  • Vendor economies

Britain does not control faction land.

Britain controls:

  • Legitimacy
  • Trade access
  • Legal recognition
  • Narrative authority
  • Who is "lawful" vs "criminal"

That's far more powerful ‚Äî and far more fragile.

Factions as World Actors

Every faction that controls territory exists in one of three realm-facing states relative to Britain.

These are not toggles.
They are stances recognized (or not) by the Crown.

1Ô∏è‚É£ Recognized Faction (Aligned / Chartered)

This is your "loyal Cove" case.

A faction controlling territory (ex: Cove, Buccaneer's Den, Orc Fort) may petition Britain for recognition.

What Recognition Means

  • Britain acknowledges the faction leader as the responsible authority for that territory
  • Britain agrees to treat faction warrants, contracts, and officials as legitimate

The faction may:

  • Operate openly
  • Trade in Britain
  • Petition court
  • Receive protection (explicit or implied)

What Britain gets:

  • Taxation or tribute, negotiated (not imposed)
  • Political leverage ("we recognized you ‚Äî we can withdraw it")
  • Stability without occupation

This is not ownership.
It is charter.

A recognized faction is effectively a licensed power.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Tolerated Faction (Neutral / Unaligned)

This is your gray zone.

The faction:

  • Controls territory
  • Pays no tribute
  • Owes no loyalty
  • Receives no protection

Britain:

  • Does not recognize their authority
  • Does not actively suppress them
  • Treats them as foreign or extralegal

What This Creates

  • Smugglers
  • Mercenary hubs
  • Pirate ports
  • Radical enclaves
  • "We don't answer to Britain" RP

This is incredibly important because it allows:

  • Non-binary politics
  • Slow escalation
  • Faction identity without instant war

Toleration is Britain admitting it cannot‚Äîor will not‚Äîpay the cost to suppress you.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Outlawed Faction (Rebel / Illegitimate)

This is open defiance.

Britain publicly declares:

  • The faction's authority invalid
  • Its leader criminal, pretender, or enemy of the realm

What Changes Mechanically (Without New Systems)

Britain refuses to recognize:

  • Faction contracts
  • Faction warrants
  • Faction legal claims

Britain may:

  • Sanction trade
  • Encourage rival factions
  • Deputize loyalists
  • Place bounties
  • Justify violence politically

The faction still exists.
Its stone still exists.
Its property still exists.

But it lives outside the law.

This is rebellion without flag-flipping.

The Cove Example (Using Only What You Already Have)

Scenario A: Loyal Cove

A Pirate or Merchant faction controls Cove

They negotiate recognition

Britain:

  • Recognizes their leader as "Warden of Cove" (title is cosmetic)
  • Collects a % of Cove's vendor taxes
  • Defends Cove politically

Result:

  • Cove is prosperous but controversial
  • Opponents call them puppets
  • Internal faction dissent becomes content

Scenario B: Neutral Cove

Cove faction refuses recognition

Britain tolerates them

Result:

  • Cove becomes a haven for:
    • Smugglers
    • Exiles
    • Mercenaries
  • Britain applies indirect pressure
  • Players decide if neutrality is worth the risk

Scenario C: Rebel Cove

Cove faction declares Britain illegitimate

Britain outlaws them

Result:

  • Trade embargoes
  • Bounties
  • Rival factions encouraged to contest Cove
  • Britain-backed "legitimate claimant" RP emerges

No new mechanics required.
Only recognition state.

Why This Is Better Than a Barony System

Adding "baronies" would just duplicate what factions already do.

Instead, you let Britain's court be the layer that decides what factions mean.
