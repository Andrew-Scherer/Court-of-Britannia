What Are Guilds?

A guild can also be considered an Organization, Group, Religion, Political Body, or any other group of members that rallies under the same cause and banner.

Guilds allow players to not only turn themselves into a formidable group, but it also allows them to control a single guild house in a more permanent way.

A guild is where your legacy lives beyond your character.

Why Guilds Matter on a Permadeath Shard

In a world where characters can die permanently, guilds provide:

  • A central gathering point ‚Äî Guilds can't "mechanically" begin unless they control a property to place their guildstone within
  • Continuity ‚Äî The organization survives individual deaths
  • Legacy ‚Äî Your accomplishments outlive your character
  • Collective power ‚Äî Groups can accomplish what individuals cannot
  • Memory ‚Äî Stories and rivalries persist across generations of characters

When your character dies, your guild remembers. Your enemies remember. Your stories persist.

Creating Player Guilds

Players can create their own guilds through a two-stage process:

Stage A: Unrecognized Guild (No Mechanics)

Players can start a guild socially any time:

  • "We are The Order of the Eternal Flame now."
  • They wear matching colors
  • They claim a tavern table
  • They write a charter and share it with their members

Mechanically, it's just roleplay, not quite set in stone yet.

Stage B: Recognized Guild

To become official, they must:

  • Muster up enough gold to collectively purchase a property
  • Buy a guildstone and place it inside the property
  • A leader is announced (or not)

This is where mechanics engage.
