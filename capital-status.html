Where New Life Begins

The capital is where the realm lives.
Authority does not die by decree. It dies when people leave.

What Capital Status Means

Capital status is not ceremonial. It is not symbolic. It is the mechanical anchor point where new characters enter the world.

The Capital is:

  • The default spawn point for all new characters
  • The city whose ruler is treated as First Among Crowns
  • The center around which other cities orient for legitimacy and protection
  • The city that can receive fealty from other cities

The Capital is NOT:

  • ‚ùå A title granted by admin
  • ‚ùå A flag on a building
  • ‚ùå A permanent status
  • ‚ùå Immune to collapse

The Core Mechanic: Spawn Gravity

Capital status exists for one mechanical reason: to determine where new players spawn.

Everything else is political consequence.

New Character Spawn Rule

When a player creates a new character, they spawn in the designated Capital city.

Not Britain by default.
Not their choice.
The Capital.

Why This Matters

Spawn gravity creates natural population flow:

  • New players enter at the center of political power
  • Cities compete to become the Capital to attract population
  • Losing Capital status means losing new player influx
  • Migration becomes visible and dramatic

This single mechanic turns population into power, and power into population.

How Capital Status Is Determined

Capital status is determined by resident count alone.

The Capital Eligibility Threshold

A city must have 40+ residents to be eligible to become Capital.

Britain starts as Capital by default. It holds that status until another city crosses the threshold and claims it.

Capital Transition Rules

Automatic Claim: When a city crosses 40 residents and no other Capital-eligible city exists, it may claim Capital status.

Contested Claim: If multiple cities are Capital-eligible, the city with the highest resident count holds Capital status.

Retention Buffer: Once a city becomes Capital, it retains that status until it drops below 35 residents (hysteresis prevents flapping).

Britain's Special Status

Britain is the default Capital, but not the permanent Capital.

Britain is immortal as a system, but not as a power.

Capital Transition Process

What happens when Capital status shifts?

  1. Yew crosses 40 residents ‚Äî Becomes Capital-eligible
  2. Threshold maintained for 3 days ‚Äî Stability verification
  3. Realm-wide announcement fires:

  4. Spawn point shifts immediately ‚Äî New characters spawn in Yew
  5. Britain loses Capital status ‚Äî Becomes a normal city-state
  6. Fealty relationships re-anchor ‚Äî Vassals now oriented toward Yew
  7. Political legitimacy shifts ‚Äî Yew's ruler becomes First Among Crowns
No coronation required. No vote. No admin toggle.
The world simply accepts reality: power has moved.

What Capital Status Does NOT Affect

Becoming Capital does not grant:

  • ‚ùå More office slots than Active cities
  • ‚ùå Higher salary caps
  • ‚ùå Stronger guards
  • ‚ùå Automatic wealth
  • ‚ùå Immunity from attack
  • ‚ùå Control over other cities' internal politics

Capital status is political recognition, not mechanical advantage.

Multiple Capitals (Edge Cases)

What If Two Cities Both Have 40+ Residents?

The city with the higher resident count holds Capital status.

If tied exactly:

  • Current Capital retains status (inertia favors stability)
  • If no current Capital, Britain defaults

Can There Be Two Competing Capitals?

Mechanically: No. Only one city can be the spawn point.

Politically: Yes. A city might claim to be the "true Capital" even if it lacks mechanical status. This is rebellion/civil war territory.

Political vs Mechanical Capital

Mechanical Capital : Where new players spawn. Determined by population.

Political Capital: Where rulers claim legitimacy. Determined by narrative and conflict.

These can diverge, creating rich drama.

Losing Capital Status

A Capital can lose its status through population collapse.

How Britain Can Fall

  1. Residents leave due to oppression, taxes, or rival opportunities
  2. Population drops below 40
  3. Retains Capital status temporarily (35-resident buffer)
  4. If population continues dropping below 35 residents
  5. Another city with 40+ residents automatically becomes Capital
  6. Spawn point shifts
  7. Britain becomes a normal city-state
This is not a bug. This is the endgame.
When a Capital loses its people, it loses the realm.

Spawn Rules (Detailed)

New Character Creation

When a player creates a new character:

  • Character spawns in the current Capital city
  • Spawns at a designated "arrival point" (inn, gate, town square)
  • Is automatically declared a resident of that city
  • Subject to that city's laws and taxes immediately

Death and Respawn

This is a permadeath shard. Characters do not respawn after death.

Capital status only affects initial character creation, not resurrection (which does not exist).

What If There Is No Capital Above Threshold?

Britain is the failsafe.

If no city meets the Capital threshold (40+ residents):

  • New players spawn in Britain by default
  • Britain retains Capital status until another city qualifies
  • This ensures new players always have a spawn point

Migration Pressure from Spawn Gravity

Capital status creates self-reinforcing migration loops:

Positive Feedback Loop (Growth)

  1. City gains residents
  2. Crosses 40-resident threshold
  3. Becomes Capital
  4. New players spawn there
  5. Population increases further
  6. City strengthens

Negative Feedback Loop (Decline)

  1. Capital oppresses residents
  2. Residents migrate to rival city
  3. Population drops below threshold
  4. Loses Capital status
  5. New players stop arriving
  6. City weakens further

This makes governance matter. Rulers who fail to retain residents lose everything.

Common Questions

Q: Can I choose where I spawn as a new character?

A: No. You spawn in the current Capital. You may migrate immediately after, but initial spawn is automatic.

Q: If Britain loses Capital status, what happens to existing residents?

A: Nothing. They remain residents of Britain. They can stay or migrate as they choose.

Q: Can a city claim Capital status through conquest?

A: Not mechanically. Capital status is determined by resident count. However, a city might politically claim to be "the true Capital" and enforce that through violence.

Q: What if the Capital is hostile to new players?

A: They spawn there anyway. This creates drama ‚Äî new players must navigate a dangerous regime, seek refuge, or adapt. It's content, not a problem.

Q: Can there be rotating Capitals?

A: Only if population naturally shifts. There is no timer or rotation system ‚Äî only population counts.

Q: Does the Capital pay higher upkeep costs?

A: No. There is no mechanical penalty or bonus for being Capital. The consequences are entirely political.

Design Intent

Capital status is the bridge between mechanical systems and political narrative.

  • Spawn gravity ensures the Capital matters mechanically
  • Population threshold ensures only stable cities can claim it
  • Automatic transition removes admin from the equation
  • No bonuses keeps it purely political, not mechanical
  • Britain failsafe prevents new player lockout
Capital status is not a reward. It is a target.
