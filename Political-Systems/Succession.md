## The Coronation Timer: Pressure Cooker

The moment the King dies and a Regent exists, a **coronation window** begins (typically 3-7 real days).


> **â— Note**
> - There will be a coronation
> - Someone will be crowned
> - Or blood will be spilled


### What Can Happen During the Window


| Actor | Possible Actions |
|---|---|
| Heir | Secure protection, build alliances, survive assassination attempts |
| Regent | Govern, delay coronation (once), advance it (risky), or crown themselves |
| Claimants | Press claims, gather support, challenge legitimacy, plot coups |
| Marshal | Choose sides, protect Heir or back Regent, remain neutral |
| Nobles | Support various factions, negotiate deals, position for power |

### It Preserves:


- **Succession tension** â€” Nothing is automatic

## Possible Outcomes


### Scenario 1: Peaceful Succession


1. King dies, Regent assumes power
2. Heir is protected during coronation window
3. Regent officiates coronation for Heir
4. Heir crowned, becomes King
5. Regent authority dissolves
6. **Stable transition**


### Scenario 2: Regent Usurpation


1. King dies, Regent assumes power
2. Regent delays coronation, builds support
3. Heir becomes vulnerable
4. Regent crowns themselves at coronation
5. Heir's claim invalidated (or Heir eliminated)
6. **Contested reign begins**


### Scenario 3: Claimant Uprising


1. King dies, Regent assumes power
2. Claimant gathers supporters
3. Challenges legitimacy of Heir
4. Forces way into coronation or disrupts it
5. Crowns themselves or backs different candidate
6. **Civil war potential**


### Scenario 4: Heir Assassination


1. King dies, Regent assumes power
2. Heir is assassinated during window
3. Succession crisis deepens
4. Regent must choose new heir or crown themselves
5. Claimants emerge
6. **Maximum chaos**
