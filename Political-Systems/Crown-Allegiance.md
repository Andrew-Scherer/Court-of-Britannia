# The Social Contract: Civilization vs. Wilderness

Britannia is not a single unified state. It is a **Two Civilizations** system.

The "Crown" does not claim universal dominion over every inch of soil. It claims dominion over **Civilization**. The rest is the **Wilderness**.

Every Guild and Property Owner faces a fundamental choice: **Allegiance** or **Independence**.

## 3. The Mechanics of Allegiance

### Buying Legitimacy
Allegiance is not just roleplay; it is a **subscription to civilization**.
*   **By Guild:** A Guild Leader declares allegiance for the entire organization.
*   **By Household:** A Property Owner declares allegiance for their land and co-owners.

### The Benefits Explained
1.  **The Shield of Law:** If a Vassal is killed, the Crown records it as **Murder**. The killer gains a Murder Count and a Bounty. The Crown enforces consequences for your death.
2.  **The Power of Trade:** Only Vassals can access the **Clearinghouse Logistics**. Non-aligned entities are invisible to the global supply chain; they must rely on foot traffic to their physical vendors.
3.  **The Safety of Gold:** Only Vassals can use the **City Bank** network. Non-aligned players must store wealth in vulnerable Guild Vaults or Housing storage.

## 4. Renouncement & Rebellion

A player or guild may **Renounce** the Crown at any time. Breaking the contract is an act of supreme agency.

### The "Debt Lock-In" Rule
Renouncing the Crown stops *new* taxes from accruing, but it **does not erase the past**.
*   **Pre-Renouncement Debt:** Any gold owed to the Crown *at the moment of renouncement* is **Locked In**.
*   **Persistent Liability:** The Crown retains the right to Seizure for this specific debt forever.
*   **Anti-Exploit:** You cannot rack up loans and then "quit" to escape them.

*   **The Result:** You will likely be killed and looted before you reach the gates. This is a valid, high-stakes political statement (or suicide).
