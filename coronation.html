Core Principle

The Only Mechanical Gate

The coronation ceremony is the single moment where Crown authority transfers from vacancy to a living sovereign. There is no King until someone completes this ritual.

This is not automatic. This is not guaranteed. This is earned through presence, timing, and survival.

The coronation is designed to be:

  • Ceremonial ‚Äî Feels ritualistic and meaningful
  • High-stakes ‚Äî Vulnerable, interruptible, and contested
  • Immersive ‚Äî Players witness the moment power changes hands
  • Political ‚Äî Forces alignment, protection, and betrayal into one moment

The Cost of Spectacle

Coronations Drain the Treasury

Coronations are costly to the Crown and carry not just a political price, but a financial burden as well. The spectacle, ceremony, and public celebration required to legitimize a new sovereign depletes royal resources.

Mechanical Effect: A coronation automatically reduces the Crown's coffers by XX% when it occurs.

This cost represents:

  • Ceremonial arrangements and preparations
  • Public feasts and celebrations to maintain legitimacy
  • Gifts and favors to secure noble support
  • The inherent expense of transferring power

This drain applies regardless of who is crowned‚ÄîHeir, Regent, or Claimant. Power is expensive.

The Royal Throne

Britain Castle Throne

Location: Britain Castle Throne Room, elevated at the head of the grand chamber

Appearance: An ornate throne draped in red velvet, positioned on a raised platform with steps leading up to it

When Inactive: The throne sits empty, a symbol of vacant power

When Active: The throne glows with golden light, calling for a sovereign

The Coronation Ritual

Step 1: Approach the Throne

When the throne is active (glowing), any player may approach and attempt to sit upon it.

No restrictions. Heir, Regent, Claimant, or common citizen‚Äîall may attempt.

Step 2: Sit Upon the Throne

To become King, a player must sit on the throne for 15 seconds uninterrupted.

Requirements:

  • Must remain seated for the full 15 seconds
  • Cannot take any damage
  • Cannot move or stand up
  • If damaged OR if the player moves, the coronation is interrupted

Step 3: The Oath of Sovereignty

While sitting on the throne, the player's character automatically speaks the oath of sovereignty (displayed overhead and in system messages):

"On this day forward, I, <Player Name>, pledge to serve and protect Britain to the best of my abilities. I shall uphold the laws of Virtue, defend the realm from those who would see it fall, and rule with justice and strength. By my blood and honor, I claim the Crown."

Duration: 15 seconds (oath completes when the timer ends)

Step 4: Interruption or Success

If the player takes damage OR moves during the 15 seconds:

  • Coronation is immediately interrupted
  • Player stands up from the throne
  • The throne remains active‚Äîanyone can try again
  • System message: "The coronation has been disrupted!"

If the player completes the full 15 seconds without damage or movement:

  • Coronation succeeds
  • Player is granted the King role
  • Server-wide announcement: "<Player Name> has been crowned King of Britain!"
  • Visual effect: Golden burst of light, bell tolls
  • The throne becomes inactive
  • All Regent authority immediately dissolves

Edge Cases & Special Situations

What If No One Completes Coronation? (45 Minutes Expire)

If the 45-minute window expires without a successful coronation:

  • Regal object deactivates
  • Server announcement: "The Crown remains unclaimed. Britain enters deeper into chaos."
  • A new 3-day timer begins automatically
  • Regent authority continues (if a Regent exists)
  • Succession crisis intensifies

This can repeat indefinitely until someone is crowned or the community agrees to reset.

What If Multiple People Try at the Same Time?

Answer: Only one player can initiate coronation at a time.

  • The first player to double-click begins the ritual
  • All other attempts during those 15 seconds are blocked
  • If the first attempt is interrupted, the next person can immediately try

What If the Heir/Claimant Dies During the Window?

Death during the 45-minute window does not affect coronation mechanics:

  • If they die before attempting coronation ‚Üí they're out of contention
  • If they die during the 15-second oath ‚Üí coronation is interrupted, someone else can try
  • Permadeath applies normally‚Äîthey cannot come back

What If No One Shows Up? (Empty Throne Room)

If no one enters the throne room during the 45-minute window:

  • Timer expires, new 3-day timer begins
  • This can happen due to fear, apathy, or strategic delay by Regent
  • Realm remains in Interregnum‚Äîno King exists

Can Guards Interrupt Coronation?

Yes. Guards can attack during the 15-second oath to interrupt it.

  • This allows Regent to block Heir coronations
  • Creates dramatic tension: will guards obey orders or side with the Heir?
  • Guard loyalty becomes the deciding factor
