You Occupy History, Not Build It

The Core Conclusion

Players do not build new structures.
They occupy existing ones.
Custom house placement is disabled.

There are:

  • No housing deeds
  • No free-placement construction
  • No plot claiming
  • No blueprint-based building

Where Players Live

Players live in:

  • Existing buildings ‚Äî Already on the map
  • City residences ‚Äî Homes, shops, inns within Britain
  • Historic properties ‚Äî Forts, keeps, monasteries, coves
  • Embedded structures ‚Äî Halls, camps, outposts

Every Place Worth Holding Already Exists

You do not build a fort.
You win one at auction.

You do not construct a keep.
You bid for one.

You do not place a house.
You acquire one.

Why This Matters

This makes:

  • Geography finite and contested
  • Space meaningful and visible
  • Ownership a statement
  • History persistent
  • Power tied to real places

Geography Is Finite

In traditional UO:

  • Custom housing spreads endlessly
  • Maps become cluttered
  • Meaningful locations get obscured
  • Geography loses structure

Here:

  • Every property is hand-placed
  • The map is intentional
  • Location matters
  • Scarcity is real

Space Is Meaningful

Because housing cannot appear anywhere:

  • Zones have identity
  • Neighborhoods exist
  • Borders matter
  • Travel routes are contested
Power comes from occupying meaningful locations,
not from creating new ones.

Ownership Is Visible

Player-placed housing creates visual chaos.

Fixed housing creates:

  • Recognizable landmarks
  • Understandable political geography
  • Clear territorial claims
  • World coherence

When someone says "the Orc Fort," everyone knows where that is.
When someone says "guild hall 47," no one does.

History Persists

Custom housing creates:

  • Disposable spaces
  • Meaningless churn
  • No memory

Fixed housing creates:

  • Permanent sites of power
  • Contested legacies
  • World memory

The World Remembers

The Pirate Cove outlives every guild that claims it.
The Orc Fort exists before and after your occupation.
The Undead Keep waits for the next claimant.

Places are permanent. Guilds are temporary.

What About Expression?

Players can still express themselves:

  • Decoration ‚Äî Free access to decorative items
  • Furniture ‚Äî Arrange interiors however you want
  • Vendors ‚Äî Place merchants and shops
  • Ambiance ‚Äî Lighting, props, atmosphere

What's removed is not expression.
It's construction.

You don't need to build a castle
to make a castle feel like yours.

Britanniais Not Empty Land

Britannia is not empty land waiting to be filled.

It is a world with history.

Players move into that history.
They do not redraw it.

This is intentional.
This is fundamental.

  • Public Auction ‚Äî When properties become vacant
  • Crown Sale ‚Äî City properties periodically auctioned
  • Guild Collapse ‚Äî Historic properties returned to auction

The Design Philosophy

This choice reflects a core principle:

The World Is Not A Blank Canvas

Britannia existed before players arrived.

It will exist after they leave.

Players are temporary occupants of permanent places.

That is the story this shard tells.
