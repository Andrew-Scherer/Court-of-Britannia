## Why This Matters

This makes:


- Geography finite and contested
- Space meaningful and visible
- Ownership a statement
- History persistent
- Power tied to real places


### Geography Is Finite

In traditional UO:


- Custom housing spreads endlessly
- Maps become cluttered
- Meaningful locations get obscured
- Geography loses structure

Here:


- Every property is hand-placed
- The map is intentional
- Location matters
- Scarcity is real


### Space Is Meaningful

Because housing cannot appear anywhere:


- Zones have identity
- Neighborhoods exist
- Borders matter
- Travel routes are contested


> Power comes from occupying meaningful locations,not from creating new ones.


### Ownership Is Visible

Player-placed housing creates visual chaos.

Fixed housing creates:


- Recognizable landmarks
- Understandable political geography
- Clear territorial claims
- World coherence

When someone says "the Orc Fort," everyone knows where that is.  

        When someone says "guild hall 47," no one does.


### History Persists

Custom housing creates:


- Disposable spaces
- Meaningless churn
- No memory

Fixed housing creates:


- Permanent sites of power
- Contested legacies
- World memory


> **Note**
> **The World Remembers**
>
> The Pirate Cove outlives every guild that claims it.  
> 
>             The Orc Fort exists before and after your occupation.  
> 
>             The Undead Keep waits for the next claimant.
> **Places are permanent. Guilds are temporary.**
