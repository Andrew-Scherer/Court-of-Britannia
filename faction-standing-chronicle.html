Two ledgers. Two purposes. Never merged.

Register of Standing

What It Is

A live, authoritative snapshot of Britain's current position and stance when it comes to factions.

It answers one question only:

"As of this moment, how does Britain treat you?"

What It Shows (ONLY Current State)

  • Recognized factions
  • Tolerated factions
  • Outlawed factions
  • Current fealty relationships
  • Current protectorate assignments (if any)

What It Explicitly Does NOT Show

  • ‚ùå Past recognition
  • ‚ùå Old fealty
  • ‚ùå Who betrayed whom last year
  • ‚ùå Why something changed

No history. No narrative. No explanation.

Just status.

Why This Must Exist Separately

  • Players need instant clarity
  • Guards need to know who's lawful
  • Merchants need to know who's safe
  • Violence needs political framing now

Design Principle

This ledger is binary, cold, and cruel.

Recognition is not force.
It is permission for others to treat power as real.

Interfaction Chronicle

What It Is

A permanent, append-only historical record.

It answers a different question:

"How did we get here?"

What It Records

  • Faction fealty sworn / broken (with dates)
  • Tribute compliance over time
  • Recognition changes (granted, revoked)
  • Interventions
  • Withdrawals of protection
  • Public justifications
  • Official accusations

What It Never Does

  • ‚ùå Declare current legality
  • ‚ùå Override the Register of Standing
  • ‚ùå Simplify history

Design Principle

This ledger is slow, contextual, and damning.

Why This Must Remain Separate

  • Reputation needs duration
  • Betrayal needs memory
  • Patterns matter
  • "This was a mistake" becomes provable

This is where:

  • Factions plan long-term
  • Diplomats argue precedent
  • Rebels justify action
  • Loyalists point to receipts

History is ammunition
and this is the armory.

How They Work Together (Critical)

They must cross-reference, but never merge.

Example: Faction X

  • 1487: Partial tribute (3x)
  • 1488: Protection requested, no response
  • 1488: Recognition revoked
  • 1488: Declared outlaw

This creates:

  • Immediate clarity
  • Long-term consequence
  • Political debate
  • And zero enforcement scripts

Why Merging Them Would Be a Mistake

If you merge them:

  • The present gets muddy
  • History becomes cluttered
  • New players get lost
  • Legitimacy becomes arguable instead of visible

What You Want

  • One place to check
  • One place to argue

Those are different needs.

Final Canon Lock-In

Design Canon (Use Everywhere)

The Register of Standing tells you who counts today.
The Interfaction Chronicle tells you who can be trusted tomorrow.

They are siblings.
They are not twins.

Summary

The Register of Standing is Britain's current, authoritative declaration of how factions are treated ‚Äî recognized, tolerated, or outlawed. It provides no history, no justification, only status. Players, guards, and merchants check it for immediate clarity.

The Interfaction Chronicle is a permanent historical record of fealty changes, tribute compliance, recognition grants and revocations, and official justifications. It provides context, patterns, and precedent, but never declares current legality.

They must cross-reference but never merge. One tells you who counts today. The other tells you who can be trusted tomorrow.
