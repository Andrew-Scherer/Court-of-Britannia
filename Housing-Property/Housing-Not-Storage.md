## The Core Conclusion


> Houses are not vaults.They are places to live.

Housing does not function as static storage.

Players cannot use housing for:


- Permanent storage of secured containers
- Lockdowns that persist indefinitely
- Hoarding mechanisms
- Insulating progress from risk


## What This Removes

Traditional housing storage creates:


- **Hoarding Anxiety** â€” Fear of losing accumulated items creates paranoia and defensiveness
- **Lock/Permission Micromanagement** â€” Complex trust systems and access control become necessary
- **Shell-Guild Storage Exploits** â€” Players fragment into single-person guilds to maximize storage
- **Housing-as-Entrenchment Gameplay** â€” Power accumulates behind walls instead of in the world

By removing housing storage, you eliminate all of these problems.


## Housing Grants Access, Not Ownership


> **Note**
> **How It Works**
>
> Living in a building grants **access to your global bank box**, not ownership of storage containers.
> **Residency â†’ Access to banking services**

Personal items are stored through the **banking system**.

Storage is:


- **Individual** â€” Each player has their own bank box
- **Private** â€” No one else can access your items
- **Abstracted** â€” Not tied to the building itself


## Why This Works

This design allows:


- Shared housing without forced trust
- Cohabitation without theft risk
- Political loss without inventory wipeouts
- Multiple residents without permission complexity
- Cooperation without vulnerability
