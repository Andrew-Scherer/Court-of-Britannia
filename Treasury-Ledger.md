## What the Royal Treasury Ledger Is

The Royal Treasury Ledger is a **read-only financial record**. It reflects the current and historical state of the city treasury. It exists independently of who is online. It updates automatically through system events.

**Operational Rules:**

- No manual entries
- No discretionary edits
- No retroactive corrections

The ledger observes reality. It does not construct it.

## What the Ledger Shows (Authoritative Scope)

The Master of the Treasury and the Crown have full read access to the following information:

### Current State

- **Current treasury balance** â€” the gold available at this moment

### Inflow

- **Daily tax revenue (aggregated):** Total collected from all tax categories
- **NPC fees and premiums:** Revenue captured from system purchases
- **Property-related income:** Taxes on land sales and transfers
- **Other system-generated revenue:** Fines, bounties, and miscellaneous city income

### Outflow

- **Daily wage payouts:** Actual gold paid to all seated roles
- **Bounty payments:** Rewards distributed through the justice system
- **Other automatic system sinks:** Governance costs and city expenses

### Projection Layer

- **Payroll projections based on filled roles:** Expected daily wage obligations under current staffing
- **Deficit forecasts under current policy:** Projected timeline to shortfall if revenue trends continue
- **Estimated days until partial or failed wages:** Warning threshold for insolvency

**Critical Limitation:**

Forecasts are informational. They do not block policy. They do not force action. They provide visibility, not control.

## Difference Between the Treasury Ledger and the Tax Ledger

The Royal Treasury Ledger and the Tax Ledger serve distinct constitutional functions. They are complementary, not redundant.

| Tax Ledger | Royal Treasury Ledger |
|------------|----------------------|
| Records policy | Records outcomes |
| Shows rates | Shows balances |
| Logs proposals and changes | Tracks what remains |
| Tracks what is being taken | Reveals whether policy worked |
| Public access | Restricted access |
| Documents authority | Documents consequence |

> **Constitutional Distinction:**
>
> "The Tax Ledger records intention. The Treasury Ledger records reality."

A city may set aggressive tax policy (recorded in the Tax Ledger) and still face insolvency (recorded in the Treasury Ledger). The Tax Ledger shows what was chosen. The Treasury Ledger shows what happened.

## Offline Continuity Rule

The Royal Treasury Ledger operates continuously, regardless of player presence.

**While No One Is Online:**

- Ledger updates continue
- Revenue accrues automatically
- Wages attempt to pay on schedule
- Shortfalls record automatically
- Projections recalculate based on current data

**When Players Return:**

- They respond to recorded reality
- They do not decide whether events occurred
- They do not retroactively adjust outcomes

The city's fiscal state does not pause when leadership is absent. The ledger reflects what happened, not what was convenient.
