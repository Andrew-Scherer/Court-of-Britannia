## What Diplomats Do

Diplomats conduct the daily work of Britain's external presence. They deliver messages and proclamations, observe and report on faction activities, attend faction events as Britain's representatives, and create the initial contact points between Britain and factions.

Diplomats operate in spaces where Guards have no authority and Military presence would escalate. They are the visible face of Britain in faction territories, relying on diplomatic protection and Britain's reputation to stay safe.


> They are not powerful. They are dangerous to touch.

## Relationship With Other Roles


### Envoys

Diplomats report to Envoys who:


- Assign specific missions and territories
- Provide direction on proclamations to deliver
- Receive intelligence reports from the field
- Appoint and dismiss Diplomats


### High Emissary

The High Emissary sets overall diplomatic strategy. While Diplomats rarely interact directly with the High Emissary, their work implements the High Emissary's vision for Britain's external relationships.


### Guards and Military

Diplomats coordinate with Guards and Military when:


- **Tax enforcement escalates** â€” Guards provide backing for seizures
- **Escort is needed** â€” Military protects diplomats traveling through hostile territories
- **Intelligence is critical** â€” Diplomats warn of imminent attacks

- Military action undermines ongoing diplomatic efforts
- Diplomats demand restraint; Military demands freedom of action


## Political Temptations and Risks


### Developing Faction Loyalties

Diplomats assigned to specific factions over time may develop loyalty to those factions over Britain:


- Personal relationships with faction leaders
- Respect for faction culture and values
- Belief that Britain's policy toward the faction is unjust
- Offers of better treatment from powerful factions


> **â— Note**
> Unlike military coups, diplomatic defection operates through:
> - Selective reporting (suppressing unfavorable intelligence)
> - Delayed deliveries (buying factions time to prepare)
> - Sympathetic interpretation (presenting faction positions favorably)
> - Warning factions of Britain's intentions


### Intelligence Manipulation

Diplomats who suppress or fabricate intelligence can:


- Engineer crises by hiding warnings
- Blind Britain to faction threats
- Create false justifications for war
- Favor one claimant during succession crises

**By the time defection is obvious, Britain's external relationships have already shifted.**
