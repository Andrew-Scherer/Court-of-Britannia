Role-Based Property and the Land Authority System

The Core Principle

Property is always held by a ROLE ‚Äî sometimes individual, sometimes collective. Individuals enter and vacate roles; they do not own property outright.

Buildings do not belong to players; they pass through custodianship, succession, and legitimacy.

Property-Holding Roles

All property is tied to a role, which unifies individuals and units under the same system.

Royal Property vs Free Market Property

Properties in Britain fall into two categories based on control structure:

Royal Property (Crown-Controlled Access)

Certain prestigious or strategic properties remain under permanent Crown ownership and operate under the Royal Tenure system:

  • Ownership never transfers
  • The Crown grants conditional access, not deeds
  • Examples: Noble estates, strategic buildings, important civic structures

‚Üí See Royal Tenure System for complete mechanics of Crown-controlled property access.

Free Market Properties (Deed-Based)

Other properties are available through open systems and can be owned via deed:

  • Public auction
  • First-claim basis
  • Managed by Land Commissioner and Stewards without royal approval
  • Examples: Common cottages, shops, farms, trade spaces

The King does not control these properties.

Individual Property Roles

For small cottages, shops, and farms:

  • Farmer of East Britain
  • Cottage Holder #3
  • Shopkeeper ‚Äì South Market

These are single-seat roles. When the player dies, the system automatically clears the role and the property enters a grace period before becoming OPEN (available for reassignment). For royal properties, the King may reassign immediately.

Collective Property Roles

For noble houses, guilds, political factions, and offices:

  • Rowan House Seat ‚Äì Manor
  • Britain Guard Company Barracks
  • Hawkwright Guildhall

These have multiple residents and persist through individual deaths.

Land Authority System

Land authority is executed by Stewards and the Land Commissioner. Both may deed property, place notices, and execute evictions. The King alone issues binding orders over Royal Property and appoints the Land Commissioner. Stewards may act without authorization, but all actions are public, logged, and punishable. Mechanical power always works; legitimacy determines survival.

This is not just a housing system. It's a political violence system where buildings are the weapon.

Authority Structure

1. The King

  • Appoints and removes the Land Commissioner
  • Issues orders over Royal Property only (castles, manors, estates, major civic buildings)
  • Can retroactively legitimize or condemn land actions
  • ‚ùå Cannot fire individual Stewards (only Commissioner can)
  • ‚ùå Does not touch day-to-day land actions
  • ‚ùå Does not execute evictions himself

The King controls policy, not enforcement.

2. Land Commissioner (Senior Executor)

  • Execute evictions physically
  • Place notices on properties
  • Deed properties to new owners
  • Act on Royal Orders
  • Act without Royal Orders (at risk)
  • Hire and fire individual Stewards
  • Suspend a Steward immediately

A Steward with personnel control and extra social weight. This is a hands-on role, not manager-only.

3. Stewards (Distributed Executors)

  • Execute evictions physically
  • Place notices on properties
  • Deed properties to new owners
  • Act with or without authorization
  • Visible, fallible, corruptible, fully accountable
  • ‚ùå Cannot hire or fire other Stewards
  • Can be suspended instantly by Land Commissioner

Expendable hands with real power. That makes the role dangerous and desirable.

‚Üí See Land Commissioner & Steward Roles for role requirements and how to join.

The Legitimacy Rule

Core Truth

Stewards and Land Commissioners have mechanical power; legitimacy determines whether they survive using it.

  • Actions always "work" ‚Äî The deed transfers, the eviction executes, the notice appears
  • Judgment happens after ‚Äî Did you have authority? Was it legitimate?
  • Punishment is social, legal, or lethal ‚Äî Not mechanical rollback

This is exactly how real power works.

Abuse & Grief Safeguards

1. Full Action Logging (Non-Negotiable)

Every action records through the Land Ledger: Actor (which Steward/Commissioner), Property affected, Action type (deed, eviction, notice), Timestamp. No exceptions. This creates accountability.

2. Instant Suspension (Key Safety Valve)

The Land Commissioner can immediately suspend a Steward. Suspension removes land powers but does NOT undo past actions. This prevents ongoing damage without rewriting history.

3. Challenge Window (Time-Limited Cancellation)

Evictions, grants, and deeds have a 24-hour challenge window. During this window, the King or Magistrate can cancel the action and reverse property state. After the window closes, the action stands and punishment targets the Steward, not the land state.

Critical Rule: Firing a Steward does NOT protect them, absolve them, or reverse actions automatically. Former Stewards can be prosecuted, exiled, or executed for their actions while in office.

Real Scenario Examples

Scenario 1: Authorized Royal Eviction

  1. King publicly orders: "Evict House Hawkwright from their manor"
  2. Steward Smith physically places eviction notice on the manor door
  3. 72-hour timer starts (automated)
  4. Hawkwright has time to remove belongings OR implement some plan to get the eviction cancelled
  5. At timer end: manor becomes OPEN, access revoked
  6. Land Ledger shows: "EVICTION_EXECUTED by Steward Smith, ordered by King"
  7. Steward is safe ‚Äî acted with authority

Scenario 2: Unauthorized Eviction (Coup Attempt)

  1. Steward Jones places eviction notice on a noble house manor
  2. No King's order exists
  3. Timer starts anyway (mechanical power works)
  4. Noble house cries foul, alerts King and Land Commissioner
  5. Within 72-hour eviction window: King cancels eviction
  6. Land Commissioner suspends Steward Jones immediately
  7. Magistrate prosecutes Jones for unauthorized action
  8. Jones faces exile, execution, other punishment, or simply runs away avoiding punishment altogether

Scenario 3: Corrupt Deed Transfer

  1. Steward Brown deeds a cottage to their friend
  2. Cottage was not OPEN ‚Äî someone else had claim
  3. Original claimant protests
  4. Land Commissioner investigates, sees irregularity
  5. Within 24 hours: Commissioner cancels deed, suspends Brown. If 24 hours passes, an eviction will be required.
  6. Cottage re-deeded to rightful claimant
  7. Brown dismissed, fined, or jailed depending on severity

Scenario 4: Commissioner Overreach

  1. Land Commissioner evicts a noble without King's order
  2. Eviction executes (Commissioner has mechanical power)
  3. King did not authorize this
  4. King publicly condemns action
  5. King fires Land Commissioner, replaces with someone more honest/loyal to him
  6. Property restored to noble
  7. Former Commissioner faces trial for unauthorized action
  8. No one is above legitimacy

Building States

Every claimable building exists in one of four states:

OPEN

Vacant or available to assign. No one has recognized access. Property is ready for a new occupant.

HELD

Assigned to a role/unit. Occupants have access based on their role or membership.

FOR SALE

Currently on auction. Property will be assigned to the winning bidder at auction close.

EVICTING

Eviction timer active. Current occupants can remove belongings but no new items can be added. At timer expiry, property becomes OPEN automatically.

Collective Housing (Noble Houses, Guilds, Political Factions)

Internal Primary Selection

Noble houses control their own internal hierarchy. No King, Custodian, or Magistrate interferes. Succession inside a noble house is purely internal RP.

The city recognizes outcomes but does not adjudicate disputes.

Unit-Wide Eviction

Eviction applies to the entire unit, not individuals:

  • No partial evictions
  • Noble houses, guilds, political factions are evicted as a whole from Royal Property
  • Individuals are managed internally, not by Stewards

Death Within a Collective

When a house member dies:

  • House access remains for other residents
  • If the Primary dies, the house chooses a new one internally
  • Custodian is not involved
Key Rule: The house is attached to the noble house/unit, not the person. One death does not nuke a whole group's home.

Eviction Process

Eviction is a formal notice, not confiscation.

Phase A: Notice Period (72 hours)

  • Building stays usable
  • Evictee can freely remove items
  • No new co-owners can be added

Phase B: Access Revocation

  • At expiry, access is revoked (keys invalidated)
  • Building becomes OPEN to be assigned a new owner if it is Royal Property
  • Anything left behind is abandoned

Contents & Items

Model: "If it's in the house, it's at risk."

  • Items are physical
  • No container protection
  • No retroactive ownership claims
  • If you leave it and die, it stays ‚Äî co-owners can take it (social consequences apply)

Exile and Unit Risk (Purely RP - No Mechanics)

If a member is exiled from the city:

  • They must be removed from any unit-held residence
  • If the unit fails to remove them, the unit is harboring an exile
  • This is grounds for investigation, sanction, or eviction
Failure to self-police invites eviction.

Land Registry (Logging)

All land actions are logged:

  • Actor: Name and role
  • Action: Type (deed, eviction, notice)
  • Location: Property ID

The Land Ledger is viewable by high ranking nobles such as Magistrate, Land Commissioner, Steward, King, and Regent. The Land Ledger is data-first (server record), with physical Ledger Books as read-only terminals.

Noble house member dies House keeps property; internal selection for new Primary

Technical Implementation: Estate & Seat System

This section provides the technical architecture for implementing role-based housing in ServUO.

Core Concept: Estate ‚Üí Seat ‚Üí Character

Estate (The Building)

Each claimable building is an Estate that stores:

  • EstateId ‚Äî Unique identifier
  • HouseSerial ‚Äî Reference to BaseHouse or multi instance
  • OwningRoleId ‚Äî e.g., "TrinsicJusticiar", "RowanManor", "BritainKing"
  • ZoneDefinitions ‚Äî For complex buildings (castles)

Seat (The Role)

Each role is a Seat that stores:

  • RoleId ‚Äî Unique role identifier
  • CurrentCharacter ‚Äî Who currently inhabits the role
  • AccessGroups ‚Äî Staff, guards, etc.
  • HeirCharacter ‚Äî Optional succession rules

Transfer Process

When someone enters a role, the system runs TransferEstateToRoleHolder(roleId):

  1. Find mapped house for the role
  2. Set house.Owner to new role holder
  3. Update Co-owners/Friends lists (role-based staff)
  4. Update Bans (institutional bans persist)
  5. Update sign to show Seat name, not character name

What Actually Transfers

In UO housing systems, changing ownership affects multiple systems:

System Transfer Behavior
House Owner Changes to new role holder (primary permission source)
Co-owners/Friends Wipe individuals, keep role-based staff groups
Secures & Lockdowns Access rights update based on new ownership
Bans Persist as institutional bans (recommended)
Vendors/NPCs Persist (owned by institution) or pack up (personal)
Sign/Name Display Seat name rather than character name

Complex Buildings: Zone-Based Access

For large complexes like Britain Castle, managing access via one giant friends list creates chaos when the King dies.

The Solution: Zones + Apartments

Zone Structure (Britain Castle Example)

  • Public Zone ‚Äî Throne hall, courtyard (anyone can enter)
  • Administrative Zone ‚Äî Council chamber, treasury (King, Chancellor, Scribes)
  • Military Zone ‚Äî Armory, barracks (Marshal, Royal Guard)
  • Private Zone ‚Äî Royal quarters + noble rooms (role-specific)

Room-Level Apartments

Key individuals get rooms that function as micro-houses:

  • Chancellor's Quarters ‚Äî Owned by Chancellor role
  • Marshal's Quarters ‚Äî Owned by Marshal role
  • Royal Suite ‚Äî Owned by King role
  • Guard Barracks ‚Äî Owned by RoyalGuard group
Critical Advantage: When the King dies, only the King-level access changes. Everyone else keeps their room access. No cascade of permission updates needed.

Staff Access Layer

Define access by role groups, not individuals:

Access Group Zones Granted
RoyalFamily Public, Administrative, Private (royal suite)
RoyalGuard Public, Military, Administrative (patrol only)
CastleStaff Public, servant areas
Council Public, Administrative
Envoy Public, guest apartments (time-limited)

When someone is hired/fired, they are added/removed from the group. No manual door list updates.

ServUO Implementation Pattern

For Unused City Buildings

Instead of converting every static building into a BaseHouse multi, use Townhouse Regions:

Townhouse Region Approach

  1. Define a Region around the building footprint
  2. Add a Claim Sign at the front door
  3. Attach housing behavior to the region:
    • Allow/deny entry (doors)
    • Lockdown/secure containers inside region
    • Assign "owner" via role (not BaseHouse Owner)

Claim Sign Functionality

Each building has a sign/plaque that holds:

  • BuildingId ‚Äî Unique identifier
  • RegionBounds ‚Äî Coordinates defining the building
  • OwningRoleId ‚Äî Not PlayerSerial, but the role
  • AccessGroups ‚Äî Guards, Staff, etc.

Double-click opens a gump:

  • Claim building (if unclaimed)
  • View ownership (Seat/Role name)
  • Grant access to role groups
  • Evict/revoke (admin only)

Succession Event Hook

When a role is reassigned:

  1. System does NOT rewrite 100 building permissions
  2. Simply update RoleSeat.CurrentMobileSerial
  3. Buildings automatically "transfer" because they reference the role

Edge Cases & Safeguards

Permadeath Inside Estate

Problem: Role-holder dies inside their estate. Risk of lockout.

Solution:

  • On death: House enters Interregnum state (doors unlock OR steward NPC can open)
  • On new role holder assigned: Ownership locks back down

Personal Items Left Behind

Design choices for role-held inventories:

  • Option A: Role inventories persist with the role (institutional property)
  • Option B: Auto-pack "personal" containers into estate chest, move to neutral vault
  • Option C: Leave all items; successor inherits everything

Preventing Role-Swap Grief

If swapping into a role grants instant house control, players can exploit it.

Mitigations:

  • Require a witness (another player in official capacity)
  • Require swap at designated "Seat Hall" (throne room, court)
  • Enforce cooldown (10-30 minutes)

Low-Code Alternative

If minimal scripting is desired, emulate role ownership with Friends/Co-Owners:

  • Keep house owned by immortal "HouseAccount" character (NPC or staff)
  • When player takes role, add them to co-owner list
  • On death/succession, remove old, add new

Less "pure" architecturally, but very practical and avoids vendor quirks.
