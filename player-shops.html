Visible Economic Actors in Britain

Overview

Allowing players to buy and control shops (apothecaries, armories, tailors) inside Britain turns crafters into visible economic actors, not background NPCs.

This is a fundamental shift:

  • Shops are physical locations in the city
  • Shop ownership is public and recorded
  • Items sold carry shop provenance
  • Shops can be bought, sold, seized, or closed

Shop Provenance

Items sold through player-owned shops carry:

  • Shop name
  • City location
  • Optional crafter signature (if exceptional or commissioned)

Crafted by Maribel
Sold by The Green Flask, Britain

Shop Ownership Mechanics

Acquiring a Shop

Shops can be obtained through:

  • Purchase ‚Äî Buy an available shop location
  • Inheritance ‚Äî Receive through succession
  • Royal Grant ‚Äî Awarded by the King
  • Seizure ‚Äî Taken through legal or military action

Operating a Shop

Shop owners can:

  • Stock items (crafted or purchased)
  • Set prices
  • Control access
  • Refuse customers
  • Hire staff (future feature)

Losing a Shop

Shops can be lost through:

  • Death ‚Äî Passes to heirs or reverts to available
  • Seizure ‚Äî Royal or court order
  • Sale ‚Äî Owner sells voluntarily
  • Abandonment ‚Äî Extended inactivity

Shop Records

Like all systems in this shard, shops generate persistent public records:

  • Current owner
  • Ownership history
  • Shop type and location
  • Optional: Transaction logs (high-value items)

This creates accountability and enables investigation.

If everyone who wore armor from "The Iron Oak" was arrested during the coup, that shop's owner has questions to answer.
