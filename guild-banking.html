Two-Account System: Treasury and Upkeep Ledger

Philosophy: Why Two Accounts?

guilds maintain two separate financial constructs to prevent ambiguity, exploitation, and political circumvention:

  • Prevents Ambiguity: There is no debate about whether deposited gold is "for upkeep" or "for hoarding." Intent is explicit at the moment of deposit.
  • Prevents Hoarding Through Upkeep: The Upkeep Ledger cannot be used as a vault. Gold converted to upkeep credit is destroyed as wealth‚Äîit becomes time, not treasure.
  • Supports Political Pressure via Liens: Crown liens may intercept Treasury income to starve a faction's growth without killing its survival. This creates economic siege conditions without backdoor eviction.

This separation ensures that economic enforcement remains political pressure, not mechanical deletion.

Treasury (Bank)

Definition

The Treasury is the guild's general bank account. It functions as withdrawable, repurposable wealth.

Used For

  • Paying member salaries or rewards
  • Purchasing goods, weapons, or supplies
  • Funding war efforts or bounties
  • Commerce and trade
  • Hoarding wealth for future use

Access & Permissions

Treasury access is controlled by the guild leader via the faction stone or guild gump:

  • Leader: Full access (deposit, withdraw, view, set permissions)
  • Treasurer (designated officer): May deposit, withdraw, and view transactions
  • Standard Members: No access by default (leader may grant deposit-only or view-only permissions)

Deposits

Players interact with the faction stone or guild gump and select "Deposit to Treasury." Gold is transferred from inventory into the Treasury account.

Withdrawals

Only authorized members (leader or designated treasurer) may withdraw gold from the Treasury. Withdrawals are logged in the transaction history.

Upkeep Ledger

Definition

The Upkeep Ledger is not a bank‚Äîit is a prepaid meter. Gold deposited into the ledger is converted into upkeep credit, which is used exclusively to pay the guild's daily upkeep cost.

Key Characteristics

  • Non-Withdrawable: Once gold is added, it cannot be reclaimed, transferred, or repurposed.
  • Non-Transferable: Credit cannot be moved to another faction, guild, or player.
  • Automatic Deduction: Each day, the upkeep cost is automatically deducted from the ledger balance.
  • Capped Storage: The ledger may hold a maximum of 14 days of upkeep credit.

Why 14 Days?

A two-week cap ensures the ledger is used for survival buffer, not wealth storage. It prevents factions from bypassing Crown liens by stockpiling months of gold as "upkeep." This cap:

  • Allows reasonable planning for vacations or downtime
  • Prevents indefinite economic immunity
  • Forces factions under lien to continue generating income or negotiate

How to Add to the Ledger

Players interact with the faction stone or guild gump and select "Add to Upkeep." They drag gold from inventory, and it is immediately:

  • Removed from the world economy
  • Converted into upkeep credit (measured in days/hours)
  • Recorded in transaction history

Access & Permissions

  • Leader: May add gold to upkeep and view ledger balance
  • Designated Treasurer: May add gold to upkeep and view ledger balance
  • Standard Members: May view ledger balance (cannot add unless granted permission)

Leaders may optionally grant "add to upkeep" permission to trusted members, allowing collective responsibility for survival.

Intentional Deposits

Deposits to Treasury versus Upkeep Ledger are always intentional. There is no automatic sorting, no hidden logic, no ambiguity.

Deposit Flow

When a player opens the faction stone or guild gump, they see two distinct options:

  • "Deposit to Treasury" ‚Üí Gold enters the Treasury (withdrawable, subject to liens)
  • "Add to Upkeep" ‚Üí Gold converts to upkeep credit (non-withdrawable, protected from liens)

This design forces players to declare intent at the moment of deposit. No room for post-hoc arguments.

Records & Transparency

Both the Treasury and Upkeep Ledger maintain full transaction histories, viewable by authorized members:

Treasury Transaction Log

  • Date and time of deposit
  • Depositor name
  • Amount deposited
  • Withdrawals (who, when, how much)
  • Lien Intercepts: Amount intercepted by Crown lien, date, and decree reference

Upkeep Ledger Log

  • Date and time of upkeep payment
  • Who added credit
  • Amount added (in gold and resulting days of credit)
  • Daily upkeep deductions (automatic)
  • Remaining balance (in days/hours of coverage)

These logs provide accountability, prevent embezzlement, and offer political ammunition in disputes.

Crown Liens Interaction

A Crown Lien is a legal restriction placed on a faction stone by royal decree. It intercepts surplus revenue but cannot interfere with upkeep payments.

What a Lien Can Do

  • ‚úÖ Intercept gold deposited into the Treasury
  • ‚úÖ Intercept vendor proceeds before they reach the Treasury
  • ‚úÖ Intercept donations or income flowing into the Treasury
  • ‚úÖ Freeze discretionary spending (if configured by decree)

What a Lien Cannot Do

  • ‚ùå Touch the Upkeep Ledger
  • ‚ùå Prevent upkeep payments
  • ‚ùå Block players from adding gold directly to the Upkeep Ledger
  • ‚ùå Force upkeep failure

Why This Distinction Matters

If a lien could block upkeep, it would be backdoor eviction by decree‚Äîa mechanical deletion disguised as political pressure. Instead, liens create a siege economy:

  • The faction can survive (upkeep still payable)
  • The faction cannot grow (all surplus income seized)
  • The faction must choose: pay the Crown, negotiate, or fight

Doctrine: Crown liens restrict surplus revenue but do not prevent payment of mandatory upkeep. Failure of upkeep under lien is treated as sovereign collapse, not eviction.

Common Scenarios

Scenario 1: Member Donates Under Lien

Situation: A faction is under Crown lien. A member deposits 5,000 gold to the Treasury.

Result: The gold is immediately intercepted by the lien and applied to the faction's Crown debt. The Treasury balance does not increase. The transaction log records: "5,000g intercepted under Crown lien (Decree #47)."

Scenario 2: Paying Upkeep During Lien

Situation: The same faction needs to pay daily upkeep. A member adds 3,000 gold to the Upkeep Ledger.

Result: The gold converts to upkeep credit successfully. The lien does not intercept it. Upkeep is paid automatically the next day. The faction survives.

Scenario 3: Treasury is Empty

Situation: A guild's Treasury has 0 gold. A member wants to fund a war effort.

Result: Members must deposit gold into the Treasury. If the guild is under lien, those deposits will be intercepted. If no lien exists, deposits accumulate normally.

Scenario 4: Upkeep Ledger is Empty

Situation: A faction's Upkeep Ledger hits 0 days of credit.

Result: Upkeep fails. Property begins decay (grace period applies). Members must add gold to the Upkeep Ledger immediately to restore coverage. If property decays fully, the faction collapses‚Äîstone resets, vendors disappear, buildings become unclaimed.

Scenario 5: Ledger Hits 14-Day Cap

Situation: A faction has 14 days of upkeep credit. A member tries to add more gold to the ledger.

Result: The gump rejects the deposit: "Upkeep Ledger is at maximum capacity (14 days)." The player must either deposit to Treasury or wait until upkeep consumes some credit.

Scenario 6: Vendor Proceeds Under Lien

Situation: A faction under lien controls a vendor that makes 10,000 gold in sales.

Result: The proceeds are intercepted before they reach the Treasury. The vendor income goes directly to the Crown debt. The faction sees: "10,000g vendor proceeds intercepted under Crown lien."

Scenario 7: Leader Inactive, Ledger Empty

Situation: A faction leader goes inactive. The Upkeep Ledger has 2 days of credit left, but only the leader can add gold.

Result: If the leader did not grant treasurer permissions, the faction will collapse in 2 days. This is intentional: factions must delegate authority properly. Lack of preparation = failure.

Failure & Abuse Prevention

Anti-Exploit Rules

1. No Withdrawing from Ledger

Upkeep credit is never convertible back to gold. Once deposited, it is permanently committed to upkeep only.

2. No Laundering via Ledger

Gold added to the Upkeep Ledger cannot be transferred to another faction, guild, or player. It is consumed by upkeep or wasted if the faction collapses.

3. Lien Cannot Be Bypassed

Players cannot "trick" a lien by adding gold to the ledger beyond survival needs. The 14-day cap prevents indefinite immunity. If a faction is under lien, it must either pay Crown debt or accept economic strangulation.

4. No Ambiguous Deposits

There is no "default" account. Players must explicitly choose Treasury or Ledger. No automatic sorting, no hidden logic, no post-deposit reclassification.

5. Ledger Cap is Absolute

The 14-day maximum is non-negotiable. No faction may store more than 14 days of upkeep credit, regardless of wealth or political status.

Summary

guilds maintain two financial constructs: the Treasury (withdrawable general wealth) and the Upkeep Ledger (non-withdrawable prepaid meter). Deposits are always intentional‚Äîplayers explicitly choose which account receives gold.

The Treasury is subject to Crown liens, which may intercept income as political pressure. The Upkeep Ledger is protected from liens, ensuring that economic enforcement does not become backdoor eviction. The ledger has a 14-day cap to prevent hoarding disguised as upkeep planning.

This two-account system ensures clarity, prevents exploitation, and supports political consequences without destroying player agency or faction survival.
